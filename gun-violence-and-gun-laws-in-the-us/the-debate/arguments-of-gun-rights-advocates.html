The arguments of gun rights advocates

Gun rights activists support gun ownership for self-defense, hunting, and security against a possible tyranny of the government. Some of the main supporters of gun rights are: the Republican Party, National Association for Gun Rights, National Rifle Association, Gun Owners of America, Second Amendment Foundation, and others.

A main argument used by gun control activists is that the SecondAmendment guarantees a person’s right to own guns. In their interpretation, firearm ownership is an American tradition protected by the US Constitution. They also cite parts of the Columbia v. Heller decision which established “an individual right to possess a firearm unconnected with service in a militia, and to use that arm for traditionally lawful purposes, such as self-defense within the home.”

A second argument put forth by gun activists is that gun control laws do not prevent crime, but gun ownership does. To support this argument, they give the example of a study that found state bans on weapons did not reduce crime rates between 1980 and 2009. They also mention that most people feel safer if they own a gun.

A third argument in favor of gun rights is that gun control will not stop criminals from getting firearms and committing crimes. To support this, they mention statistics about criminals who borrowed, used, or stole guns to commit felonies.

A fourth argument used by gun rights activists is that the government will have too much power and might take away all guns from citizens. The argument is derived from a historical fear of the British Empire’s tyranny and is frequently exploited by NRA leaders.

A fifth argument is that gun control laws are an invasion of privacy. The argument refers specifically to background checks which require personal information about potential owners such as address, medical record, and criminal record.

Other arguments frequently used by gun rights activists are:

  • Gun control is unnecessary because the number of people killed by guns is relatively small compared to other death causes (diseases, car accidents, etc.).
  • Gun control does not prevent suicides, as shown by the example of countries like Lithuania, Japan, or South Korea, which have high suicide rates despite gun control laws.
  • Education about firearms and gun safety is better than gun control to prevent accidental deaths.
  • Gun control laws are ineffective and would prevent people from defending themselves from foreign invaders.
