The arguments of gun control advocates

Gun control advocates include a number of NGOs and political organizations that believe it is the responsibility of the government to prevent crime and deaths and not that of citizens. Some of the main gun control advocates are: the Democratic Party, American State Legislators for Gun Violence Prevention, Every Town for Gun Safety, Stop Handgun Violence, Faiths United to Prevent Gun Violence, Brady Campaign, and others.

The main argument of gun control advocates is that the Second Amendment does not guarantee unlimited rights regarding gun ownership. To argue for this, they give examples of various historical regulations even before the Second Amendment which were used to limit the access to guns for certain categories of people. They also cite part of the sentence in the Columbia v. Heller decision which explicitly mentions that gun rights are not unlimited.

The second argument in favor of gun control is that it would reduce the number of deaths. The argument is supported by statistics that show an increase in the number of gun-related deaths. Gun control activists also use a 2016 Lancet study which projected that gun control laws could reduce deaths by 56%.

A third argument used by gun control activists is that gun control laws could help protect vulnerable groups such as women who are victims of domestic abuse. This argument partly derives from the statistic that five women are killed with firearms every day, often during domestic disputes.

A fourth argument is that weapons are rarely used in self-defense, as suggested by statistics that show less than 1% of crimes were committed in self-defense.

A fifth argument is that convicted felons can easily have access to guns by stealing them.

A sixth argument is that gun control laws would reduce the social costs related to gun violence. These costs include legal services, medical costs, and incarceration, among others.

Other arguments mentioned by gun control activists are:

  • Most Americans, including gun owners, support common sense gun control.
  • Gun control will lead to fewer suicides and accidental deaths.
  • More people having guns makes conflicts more likely to turn violent.
  • Most massacres were committed with legally owned weapons.
