Discrimination against African Americans

Discrimination against African Americans is still common in American society.  Statistically , they are more likely to be targeted by the police and in recent years a number of unlawful or unfair police shootings of African Americans have been followed by rallies organized by groups such as Black Lives Matter. Furthermore, more African Americans than white citizens struggle economically and on average they receive poorer education.

These issues gained media and social attention after the African American man Michael Treyvon was shot in 2012, and protests intensified after the 2013 acquittal of the man who shot him. The Fergusson shooting in 2014 (an incident in which the police killed Michael Brown, an unarmed African-American teenager) also brought the issue of police discrimination to national attention.

These incidents were followed by an online campaign against racial discrimination (in Treyvon Martin's shooting case) and riots and peaceful protests against police discrimination of ethnic minorities in the US (in the Ferguson shooting case) and gave rise to the movement Black Lives Matter. 

The movement campaigns against violence and discrimination against black people by frequently organizing protests targeting police brutality and injustices in the criminal system. In just a couple of years, the movement gained global supporters, especially in other English-speaking countries such as Australia, Canada, and the UK.

The movement has succeeded in raising awareness about the fact that racial discrimination against black people is still a major issue in today’s society, especially in the justice system.

Note that changes in language show improvements regrading discrimination issues. In the decades following the civil rights movement, the language used in relation to African Americans or other minority groups changed a lot. While you might come across a text from the civil rights movement era that include words like “colored” and “negro”, these words are today considered offensive and should be avoided.

 

Discrimination against other groups

African Americans are not the only ethnic minority in the US struggling with racial discrimination. The Muslim and Latin American communities also face discrimination.

Since the 9/11 terrorist attacks, the American Muslim community has become a target of fear and discrimination among many Americans. Discrimination against Muslims in the US has been found to be increasing,  according to a Pew Research report from 2017 . According to the survey, half of Americans think Islam is not part of “mainstream American society” and 41 percent believe Islam promotes violence more than other faiths. Discrimination is also supported at a political level as indicated by the travel ban on people from a number of Muslim countries which was introduced by President Donald Trump.

Latin Americans face discrimination in the US, mostly regarding the job market. A proportion of Latin Americans living in the US are illegal immigrants and often suffer from being severely underpaid. Even immigrants who live in the US legally are still often paid significantly less than their white American counterparts. Polls from 2017 support this idea, as Latin Americans argued that they felt discriminated against when applying for jobs and in terms of salaries.

Also consider that the Juneteenth Independence Day commemorating the abolition of slavery in Southern States is still celebrated locally today, as a way to preserve a memory of the injustices of the past. Furthermore, because the celebration also reminds people of the separation of families in the past, it has also become relevant for other groups, such as Mexican illegal immigrants. Their families are also victims of forced separation, this time because of deportations during Donald Trump's presidency.
