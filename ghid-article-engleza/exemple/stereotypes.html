Stereotypes

Stereotypes is a part of everyone’s everyday life, whether they like it or not. Everyone uses it, whether they like it or not and everyone is a part of a stereotype. Whether they like it or not. The question is; is it good or bad? When you hear the definition you probably label it as a bad thing, but in some instances, it can actually be helpful. Some stereotypes can be horribly wrong, but what made people be so far off in the first place. Are there more stereotypes today, rather than for example 50 years ago?

A young man with a black hoodie and sweat pants is the only one with you on the train. He has the hood on, so you can`t see his face. It is your stop; you walk out onto the platform. Alone. He follows you. You look behind you as you walk, and he is still following you. He starts to run at you. What do you do? In this scenario, you have a choice. You can let him run; maybe he is late for his next train. Alternatively, you can run away from him, he looks like shady business. Your ethics and believes decides for you. It can save you by running away, it can make a fool out of you by running for no reason, or it can get you robbed.

Your grandmother may be old fashioned, dress differently, act differently, she might even smell a bit funny. Not judging. One thing that is quite common with older people that they think differently. If you encounter a former criminal or a person with a different background or belief, she might shrug them off and say that they are all just the same. This happens more often than not. This is because behaviour and belief were different before, racism and stereotypes have toned down in recent years. Before, with the blacks oppressed in America and foreigners looked at in disgust in Europe, people were raised to believe things that would have been unacceptable today. Unless, of course, they had money, but that is a different case. The point is, people are generally more open and has less stereotypes than before. Whether or not it is a good thing is up to you. 

A stereotype is a way to understand and learn about ones culture and people, both in a good way and in a bad way. It is a way to understand what the people are like. If you think about America, you might believe that the average American is fat, stupid, ignorant, patriotic or anything else. What if someone told you that the average American is skinny, sporty, healthy, understanding and all the good things, would you believe it or would you just shrug it off? The truth about average Joe is that he is a mixture of a lot of different people and cultures. Sure, the average weight is higher in America than the rest of the world but still, not all of them are fat and lazy. Think about Romania. Romanians are generally thought of as a sporty and skinny country. Despite that, there are many fat people here as well. 

When I hear the word American, the first word that pops into my mind is ignorant. Ignorant about every move they make both as a person and as a nation is the right move. Ignorant when it comes to other countries, they cannot imagine why the Europeans hate them so much, they do not even believe that anyone could hate them. Of course, I know that the average American has some knowledge about the world around them, but they should learn more. I do not want to sound condescending towards Americans, but their stereotype is not the best to have. I do not hate Americans, I love their culture, patriotism and style, but it seems as though it can be a bit too much sometimes. I am not the right person to ask about America, I have never been there. The only thing I can relate to is their movies and TV-shows. They have influenced the whole world with their culture. Movies, TV-shows and style. A lot of what we are using today is American. I just do not like it too American. 

You should not feel bad about stereotyping people, it happens all the time. Some people might even like to being assumed to be a tall, skinny, blond man with blue eyes. It happens to everyone, it is inevitable. However, it has to come from somewhere. Some people work actively to make people believe certain things about others, whether it to be for a laugh or for some extra views on that one segment. For example, they might have interviewed 100 people and maybe four or five of those gave hysterical answers, then the only the interviews they include are with those people. They do not intend to offend the whole nation; they assume that the people watching has enough common knowledge to understand that not everyone is like that. You cannot be (very) funny without offending someone. 

Some stereotypes are old fashioned and are terribly wrong. This is the result of lack of knowledge about the people or the country. To prevent this, schools all over the world should teach more about the world we are living in. they should teach about other countries, other cultures and other people. Too many countries care only for their own people and culture. Do not make the kids love their country, make them love what is right and what they believe is right. This is how making bad stereotypes happen, wrong schooling and raising. Help the kids make their mind up for themselves about other people and other countries. Those kids will one day be parents and they will affect their kids with their beliefs. 

At the end of the day, there is not an awful lot to do about this occurrence, people are what they are and they believe what they believe. What one might do is to teach and learn more about the culture of other people, or to try to change the minds of others. Racism plays a big part in these talks, the education and the environments one grow up in often determine ones views and beliefs. There is not much one can do about that. Stereotypes are going to stick around, at least for the near future. Whether or not this is a good thing, is up to you. Proper education can solve half the problems between people in the world. Bad stereotypes should be outdated.
