Mrs. Phelps and Mrs. Bowles

Clara Phelps and Mrs. Bowles are the two friends of Mildred Montag in Fahrenheit 451 by Ray Bradbury. They sometimes come to visit Mildred to watch television together with her (Part 2, 56%f.).

Unlikeher friends, Mrs. Bowles is the mother of two children. She presents herself as a proud mother, even though she tells in passing that her relationship with her children is cold. She thinks it's "nice" that children look like their parents and that they are useful, because otherwise humanity would die out (Part 2, 64%). At home, however, she usually just sits her children in front of the television so that she can have her peace.

Mrs. Phelps has no children and she finds the thought of having children completely crazy. She is married for the third time, but she says she leads her own life (part 2, 62%). Her husband Pete has been drafted as a soldier because there is a war approaching. 

Mrs. Phelps insists that she does not mind (part 2, 62%). She believes the propaganda that the war will soon be over. And if her husband should die, they have both agreed that she should not mourn him (ibid.).

When Guy Montag reads a poem about love and war (Part 2, 87%), Mrs. Phelps cries. This makes it very clear that she is very afraid for her husband. She has repressed this feeling. The façade of contentment is then torn down: she has only sugarcoated the circumstances.

When Mrs. Phelps bursts into tears at the reading the poem, Mrs. Bowles becomes very angry and accuses Guy of being very mean for reading the sad poem (Part 2, 87%). Guy berates her and angrily challenges her to think about her life. He knows that Mrs. Bowles has already lost some men and some have died and that she has undergone many abortions. He tells her this and thinks she should think about it and about her relationship with her unloving children. Mrs. Bowles says she will never visit this house again (Part 2, 87%).
