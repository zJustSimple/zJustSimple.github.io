Glossary

On this page you can find words that are particularly useful to know when studying Brexit. Click each word for a full definition in English.

Article 50 (s)
 

An article included in the Treaty of Lisbon which gives any EU member state the right to leave the Union with two years scheduled to negotiate an exit deal. British PM Theresa May triggered Article 50 in March 2017, placing the default deadline for a Brexit deal in March 2019. 

Brexit (s)
 

Term describing the UK’s process of leaving the EU after the 2016 referendum; combines the words "British" and "exit".

bureaucracy (s)
 

A government system in which state officials, norms and regulations seem to have more power than elected representatives. Claims of too much bureaucracy is a common point of criticism against the EU, but the Brexit process has - ironically - also created a lot of bureaucracy within the UK, as a large number of laws need to be modified. 

Conservative Party (s)
 

One of the oldest political parties in the UK. The Conservative Party supports right-wing polices such as individual entrepreneurship and the free market, and holds more conservative social views. Several members of the Conservative Party supported the Leave campaign. Members of the Conservative Party are sometimes referred to as ‘Tories’ (see below).

Euro-scepticism (s)
 

Being critical of the European Union and its policies, refusing to accept some of it rules, institutions, and even EU membership. The Brexit result showed that Euro-scepticism was very strong among the British people.

exit poll (s)
 

A survey asking people how they voted after leaving the polling station, in an attempt to predict the outcome of an election.

free movement (s)
 

Being able to move people, goods, services, workers and money from one country to another without additional taxes or restrictions. Free movement is generally allowed across the EU.

free trade (s)
 

International trade conducted without tariffs or other restrictions. The European Single Market is built on free trade.

globalisation (s)
 

A process through which business, people and organisations are more connected to the international arena and start to have influence internationally.

hard Brexit (s)
 

A Brexit in which the UK would have complete control over its borders and economy, changing to a large degree its relationship with the EU, by giving up access to the single market and the customs union.

Labour (s)
 

A common way of referring to the Labour Party in the UK. The Labour Party includes social democrats and trade unionists which promote social and leftist polices. Most Labour Party members were in favour of the UK remaining a part of the EU.

Leave (v)
 

the Brexit context, Leave refers to the campaign organised by those who wanted the UK to withdraw from the EU. Supporters of the Leave campaign included MPs from both the Conservative and the Labour parties. UKIP, a strongly right-wing party, created their own campaign, called Leave EU.

the Lisbon Treaty (s)
 

An international agreement signed in Lisbon in 2007 which updates the constitutional basis of the EU by amending the Treaty of Maastricht and the Treaty of Rome. The UK opted-out of Justice and Home Affairs regulations mentioned in the Treaty.

MP (s)
 

Member of the British Parliament. In order to sign the Brexit deal, the UK needs the approval of the majority of the MPs.

negotiations (s)
 

Talks aimed at reaching an agreement between different parties. Several negotiations were organised between the UK and the EU to reach a common Brexit agreement.

no deal (s)
 

In the Brexit context, no deal means the UK leaving the EU without a deal with the Union about what their relationship will look like after Brexit. Many politicians fear that this could have drastic consequences for both the UK economy and the British people.

opt-out (v)
 

To refuse to take part in something. In the EU countries can opt-out of certain law or treaties. For example, the UK opted-out of the Euro currency introduced by the 1992 Maastricht Treaty.

protectionism (s)
 

Economic policy through which a country tries to reduce imports by means of taxation, quotas and other government measures. Those who voted or supported leaving the EU also tend to believe that protectionist policies are better for Britain compared to free trade.

red tape (s)
 

Excessive bureaucracy to comply with rules and formalities. Many have argued that Brexit will lead to a greater amount of red tape for businesses and travellers.

referendum (s)
 

A popular vote on a single political issue, often framed as a Yes/No question. The UK organised a national referendum in 2016 asking the people whether they want the country to continue to be part of the EU or not.

Remain (v)
 

In the Brexit context, Remain refers to the campaign by those who were in favour of the UK remaining a part of the EU. The Remain supporters included most Labour MPs and several political parties in each UK country.

resign (v)
 

To willingly leave a job or an office. British Prime Minster David Cameron resigned from office after the Brexit referendum.

single market (s)
 

Countries that decide to have a common market where they can trade without limitations and tarrifs. The European Single Market was created in 1993.

snap election (s)
 

A parliamentary election organised earlier than expected. The UK Prime Minister Theresa May called for a snap election in 2018, trying to consolidate her Party’s power to negotiate Brexit with the EU. It did not work out well for her, as she ended up losing seats in parliament instead of gaining them.

soft Brexit
 

A version of Brexit in which the relationship between the UK and the EU remains relatively similar to their relationship before the UK left the EU, for example through retaining similar trade agreements.

transition period (s)
 

A time during which the state of things is changing. Brexit will be complete after a transition period which is estimated to last until 2020 or 2022.

Tory  (s)
 

Historical name for the Conservative Party and its supporters in the UK. A Tory is generally someone who values social order, traditional and conservative ideas. The Tories were split on Brexit, but many backed the Leave campaign.

UKIP (s)
 

Abbreviation for the UK Independence Party, a British political party known for its strong euro-scepticism and for promoting right-wing polices. UKIP was the main political party behind the Leave EU campaign group, but has since lost a lot of its initial support.

the United Kingdom (s)
 

State in north-west Europe, formed by four countries: England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. The United Kingdom became part of the European Union in 1973 and will leave the Union in March 2019.

Vote of no confidence (s)
 

Also called motion of no confidence. A vote of no confidence can be triggered by a Member of the British Parliament if they feel that the current government is no longer fit to rule - typically because the government has proven unable to get the required support for key proposals or policies. 

A successful vote of no confidence may trigger a general election.
