Examples

Cea mai bună modalitate de a înțelege cum trebuie redactat un eseu narativ (narrative essay) este să citești exemplele pe care ți le punem la dispoziție.

Exemplele de eseuri narative de mai jos au fost concepute de profesori, așadar te poți încrede în corectitudinea și calitatea lor.

Cuprins
  • Narrative essay: „He came upon a small opening in the floor which seemed to be a disused drainage pipe which was dry and dusty.”
  • Narrative essay: „I had never been more scared than the day I lost my little sister in a crowded mall.”
  • Narrative essay: „That sound of broken glass brought me to my senses.”
  • Narrative essay: A day in the life of a banknote

Narrative essay: „He came upon a small opening in the floor which seemed to be a disused drainage pipe which was dry and dusty.”

He came upon a small opening in the floor which seemed to be a disused drainage pipe that was dry and dusty. The pipe probably led to the old prison block on the other side of the building. In those forsaken days, it would have carried cold mountain water, mingled with the screams and sometimes blood of the unfortunate souls who were locked up in there. 

He was apprehensive of going forward, but he was filled with a sense of unshaken duty. He took a few pictures of the bleak and dilapidated room, then proceeded to the great iron door. The assembling hall was huge, and stretched on for several hundred meters, like a colossal metal cathedral, with broken stained glass on either side. Fuzzy green light poured through the checkerboard windows, illuminating the rusty machinery where thousands upon thousands of men in prison uniforms would’ve worked for twelve hours a day. 

He crossed the giant complex using the iron catwalk on the second level, and entered the gloomy dust-filled labyrinth of the prison cells. One of those cells was of great importance to him, but he would never know which one. Old communist detention centers leave no records of the individuals that perished amidst their walls. They were mere numbers on an endless black list. Nonetheless, he entered every dingy cell, and felt the silent echo of distant voices, the unshakable memory of all the men who died there so many years ago. His grandfather was one of them.

Narrative essay: „I had never been more scared than the day I lost my little sister in a crowded mall.”

I had never been more scared than the day I lost my little sister in a crowded mall. One minute she was right behind me and the next I was alone in an endless sea of people swarming in and out of the shops. Where could she have gone? I shouted her name over and over again, but to no avail. My voice was helplessly drowned by the speakers, where a woman was announcing a sale on bathrobes.

As soon as my voice broke off, I darted towards the first shop I could find in sight, ploughing my way through the crowd. They were selling Swiss chocolate and Belgian toffee. She wasn’t there. The voice from the speakers was going on about the wonderful prices on bathrobes. I ran from one toy store to the next, eyes bulging, breath failing. She was nowhere in sight. I finally stopped and took a deep breath, trying to calm myself down. I had to come up with a different approach. But how can you find a needle in a haystack? 

“New bathrobes on sale, incredible prices…” If I heard one more thing about bathrobes, I would… And then it came to me. I rushed to the info point and politely asked if I could make an announcement. A warm voice cut in on the never-ending bathrobe commercial, and my sister was kindly directed to the front entrance. I found her there, looking rather confused. “Where were you?” I asked, with a mix of annoyance and relief. “I went to check the sales on bathrobes” she answered casually. Children these days…

Narrative essay: „That sound of broken glass brought me to my senses.”

”That sound of broken glass brought me to my senses. I was snoozing in the cool summer evening, happily floating on clouds of dreamy foam, when the dreadful noise came about. I slowly rose from the bed and stumbled towards the window. There on the floor, amidst the shards of glass, lay a round gray stone, not very large, yet sufficiently sturdy to penetrate my window. 

It was probably those little rascals playing outside. I was about to give them a piece of my mind, when I noticed a scrap of paper wrapped around the stone. I picked it up carefully and read: “When one door is closed, another will open”. I was still half asleep, and the whole thing seemed so odd that I suspected I was still dreaming, so I lumbered back to bed. Hopefully, reality would soon kick in.

But as soon as I dozed off again, I heard a loud burst and the sound of glass shattering. This time I sprang up in an instant and rushed to the window. A second stone was lying on the floor, carrying – what else? – a second piece of paper. This one read: “A terrible hunger awaits all those who delay.” What on Earth was going on, and why did I keep receiving these weird proverbs? And then it dawned on me: right before going to bed I had ordered Chinese, then carelessly turned off my mobile phone and the intercom ringer. As I fought back a wave of anger, a famous saying came to mind: “fortune cookies favor the bold”. The guy deserved a tip. 

Narrative essay: A day in the life of a banknote

Fresh out of the printer, our brand new banknote takes a ride to the bank, where she and her twin sisters will eventually split ways. They are one great batch. They all share the same face and consequently, the same value. 

All of them bear the seal of greatness, the stern face of a great writer. It’s whispered that in the metal vaults below, there are artists and writers of lesser value. Humans seem to attribute value in a very arbitrary way. It used to be that their older sisters who bore the same semblance were lowest on the national scale of importance. Now they, the new lot, are top note. 

As soon as they reach the bank, a gloved man carefully handles each package in turn, and soon they find themselves occupying an even bigger metal vault. This is where their infancy ends, as some sisters start departing God knows where. 

One of them ends up in the milky-white hands of a bank employee, who displays wonderful skill and care in handling money. But this luxury will not last for long. Soon, men with dirty hands, crowded pockets, and stuffy wallets will claim her. She will mercilessly be exchanged from one hand to the next, until the broad forehead of the famous writer will be covered in grease, and its surface will darken. 

But such is her fate, and also her sisters' fate. Their endeavor is of the utmost importance, for, without them, our world would stop going round and round.
