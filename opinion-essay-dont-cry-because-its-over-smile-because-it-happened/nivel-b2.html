Whether it happens suddenly or gradually, when a relationship comes to an end, most people feel sad, hurt and even angry. When my friend Linda came to me looking for advice after a difficult, yet amicable breakup, I was not surprised by her sadness. 

But what should one say to a person in these cases? Should we encourage them to move on, or should we just allow them to cry over the situation? “Don't cry because it's over, smile because it happened” was all I could think of,  but also, I did not want my friend to think that I was insensitive to her pain. 

Firstly, she was no longer the shy person I had met years before. She had become a lively person that everyone enjoyed having around. 

Secondly, she had become a lot more confident about herself and she had recently accepted a new challenge in her professional life.
