Introduction

The idea of the American Dream is kept alive by the stories of individuals who - in one way or the other - are said to have lived the American Dream. Such stories are particularly popular within American society, and are often brought up as sources of inspiration. 

There are numerous examples of people who embody the American Dream and inspire people to believe in it, both historical and current. The list below contains just a few suggestions, we encourage you to think about other examples as well.

John D. Rockefeller

The now famous oil magnate John D. Rockefeller (1839-1937) was born into a relatively poor family in New York, with German and English roots. He started working as early as 16 to support his family. Soon, he was lending his savings to neighbors. This helped Rockefeller to eventually open his own company.

Having great analytical skills and flexibility, he began investing in other businesses. Thanks to good investments in oil, business, and industry, Rockefeller soon became one of the richest persons in the US.

Andrew Carnegie

Born in Scotland, Andrew Carnegie (1835-1919) and his family immigrated to the US because of poverty. He started working at a young age both for a telegraph company and as a factory worker.

Having a good sense for business, he gradually started investing in oil and infrastructure industry. He obtained the first big sums of money from oil speculations which allowed him to invest in the steel industry. Soon enough, he became one of the richest persons in US history. Moving from rags to riches, Carnegie also became a big philanthropist, donating much of his fortune to charitable causes.

Oprah Winfrey

Now known as one of the biggest TV celebrities in the US, Oprah Winfrey (b. 1954) is a modern example of the American Dream. She is an African-American woman who grew up in poverty in the US, raised by her grandmother in the South during segregation, and who went through troublesome teenage years, including being sexually abused.

However, the media training she received in high school paved the way to her success, landing her first TV job at the age of 19. Working her way up, Winfrey eventually became the host of an extremely popular TV show which brought her both money and influence, leading to her being occasionally ranked as the most influential woman in the world.

Arnold Schwarzenegger

Another immigrant who is living the American Dream is actor and politician Arnold Schwarzenegger (b. 1947). He was born in an Austrian village and went through a rough childhood.

He managed to move to the US in 1968 thanks to his career in competitive body building. A number of movie roles followed, making him world famous and earning him a huge fortune. By the end of the 1990s, he got involved in politics and was elected Governor of California in 2003, as a representative of the Republican Party. He has openly praised the idea of the American Dream as part of his personal success.

Elon Musk

The famous tech businessman Elon Musk (b. 1971) was born in South Africa. Teaching himself computer programming, he made it his goal to move to the US after graduating. To achieve this, he first moved to Canada in 1988. By 2002, he was already a naturalized citizen of the US. During this time, he increased his fortune by developing various tech and software companies and selling them. Today, Musk is the CEO and CTO of the private space exploration company, SpaceX, and the CEO and chief product architect of Tesla Motors.
