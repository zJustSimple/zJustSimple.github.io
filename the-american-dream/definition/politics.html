The American Dream in politics

Cuprins
  • Introduction
  • Barack Obama
  • Donald Trump

Introduction

The American Dream has been often used by politicians in their campaigns, speeches, and policies, helping us track its evolution throughout time. For example, Abraham Lincoln made the Dream more accessible to African Americans by abolishing slavery. President Woodrow Wilson extended this to women by granting them voting rights. By adopting the Civil Rights Act of 1964, President Johnson ended segregation, continuing what Lincoln started, giving further access to the American Dream for African Americans.

In political campaigns, candidates often try to describe themselves as embodiments of the American Dream, describing how they have fought hard to get to where they are - even though this often seems a bit unconvincing, as most presidential candidates have historically come from wealthy and privileged backgrounds. 

More recently, the last two American presidents, Barack Obama and Donald Trump, also explored and used their own interpretation of the American Dream to appeal to voters.

Barack Obama

While he was a Senator, Barack Obama wrote a memoir titled The Audacity of Hope: Thoughts on Reclaiming the American Dream. The book was a reinterpretation of the American Dream meant to appeal to young African Americans. Obama also presented his life as the embodiment of the American Dream. He came from a single-parent family and achieved political success through education and hard work.

In his "Reclaiming the American Dream" speech from 2007, during his presidential campaign, Obama argued that “Americans share a faith in simple dreams” and continued to say that “What is unique about America is that we want these dreams for more than ourselves - we want them for each other. That's why we call it the American dream”.  The speech is focused on the American Dream, looking at how generations of Americans built this Dream and at how Obama’s own life and background exemplifies the American Dream.

As the US President, Barack Obama believed that the following policies help keep the American Dream alive:

  • Access to universal health care
  • Reforms regarding lending practices
  • Making education accessible and affordable
  • Giving people the right to marry regardless of their sexual orientation
  • Integrating immigrants
  • Equal pay for women and men

He also continued to bring up the ideal of the American Dream in his political speeches, once again focusing on the social aspect of the Dream, and the importance of American citizens helping each other achieve it. 

Donald Trump

President Trump also put forward his version of the American Dream during his campaign and presidency. Before running for president, Trump told Forbes Magazine in 2007:

The American Dream is freedom, prosperity, peace and liberty and justice for all. That's a big dream. It's not always easy to achieve, but that's the ideal. […] We are the luckiest people in history, just by the fact that we are Americans.

During the presidential electoral campaign of 2016, Trump presented himself as a self-made businessman, arguing he became wealthy and successful through his own efforts.

However, you should note that Trump comes from a wealthy family, as his father was a rich businessman. During his presidential campaign, Trump was widely mocked for his claim to be self-made, as it was revealed that he got a 'small' loan of a million dollars from his father to get started in business. 

Some of the policies Donald Trump thinks support the American Dream are:

  • Bringing back jobs to the US to help people work their way through to success
  • Reducing taxes for companies to create a favorable context for companies to grow (and create jobs)
  • Restricting immigration to preserve more jobs and opportunities for American citizens.

However, President Donald Trump is more focused on American citizens having access to the American Dream, taking a strong position against illegal immigration especially. In his first State of the Union Address, Trump argued that “Americans are dreamers too” and that “There has never been a better time to start living the American Dream”. Trump focuses on Americans as opposed to immigrants and sees himself as someone who will restore the American Dream.
