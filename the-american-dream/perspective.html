Putting the American Dream into perspective

Cuprins
  • How the ideal of the American Dream is regarded in other nations

How the ideal of the American Dream is regarded in other nations

American ideals included under the big umbrella of the American Dream have often been used by the US to build an international reputation. One relevant example is how the US relied on the American Dream during the Cold War period. During that time, the American government invested numerous resources in public diplomacy with the purpose of undermining the Soviet culture. International visits programs, art and cultural events abroad, as well as Hollywood movies, were all meant to show the world that the US continued to be a society where the American Dream was thriving. These actions were successful in many countries, including Britain, Australia, and Canada.

Britain  was influenced by the American Dream mostly when Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher was in office. The Dream resonates with the British especially in terms of home ownership. Thatcher encouraged people to buy homes in the 1980s through various schemes for first-time buyers.

Australia  was also inspired by the American Dream in the same way as Britain. They created the Australian Dream, according to which owning a home was a path to a better life and an expression of success. Nowadays, the Australian Dream is challenged by the rising prices on the housing market compared to people’s wages. British people deal with this challenge too nowadays.

Although Canada  did not strive to compete with the US, many people believe that it is the country where the American Dream is still alive.  According to a study published in Fortune magazine , upwards social mobility is more likely in Canada than in the US:

Children in the United States have a 7.5% chance of achieving that economic mobility, according to the study. By comparison, Canadian children have a 13.5% probability. The probability for children in the United Kingdom was 9%, and in Denmark, 11.7%.

The U.S. News and World Report  also looks at how Canada is becoming more of a land of opportunity than the US, as it ranks higher among best places to live in 2018. According to the report, Canada is the second best country to live in, while the United States is ranked 7th .

In Denmark, there is an unofficial ideology which in Danish is referred to as “Janteloven”. According to this social idea, you are never allowed to feel better or more entitled than others, and you are not supposed to strive for being anything out of the ordinary. To Americans in particular, this may seem like the (negative) opposite of the American Dream which encourages personal success and frequently praises individuals who have achieved it.

Challenges around the Globe

Consumerism, wealth gaps, and social mobility are not only challenges to the American Dream. They also affect the whole world. Many studies have shown that there is a huge wealth gap between Western countries and the rest of the world, for example.

Inequality.org lists various statistics regarding this matter . For example, the website notes that “More than 70 percent of the world’s millionaires reside in Europe or North America, with 43 percent of these millionaires calling the United States home”.

An article from The Guardian  looks at a recent study that reveals the world’s wealthiest 1% own half of all wealth in the world. The same source mentions that “the poor are mostly found in developing countries, with more than 90% of adults in India and Africa having less than $10,000”.

Social mobility is also an issue in many countries.  A 2018 World Bank report  shows that Africa and South Asia have the lowest social mobility in the world and most of the world’s poorest people. The study confirms that poor and developing countries offer the least chances for people to overcome their economic status.

Consumerism is also a global problem, with the media often looking at consumerism in developed countries. There is a general belief that the more developed a country, the more likely it is to encourage consumption. However, there are also trends that are meant to discourage consumption, such as minimalism. Minimalism is a lifestyle developed in Japan which is spreading around the world, encouraging people to use and consume as little as possible.
