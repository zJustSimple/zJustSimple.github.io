Summary

“The Remains of the Day” is a novel written by British author Kazuo Ishiguro. First published in 1989, it has been awarded with the Booker Prize for Fiction that same year, thus being granted one of the highest possible distinctions given to a literary work.

The first-person narration is being told by the protagonist, Stevens. He had worked his entire life as a butler at Darlington Hall, defined by unyleding loyalty towards the master of the house, Lord Darlington. His master had recently died, so Stevens remembers him in great detail, describing him to the reader.

In 1956, Stevens receives a letter from Miss Kenton, a former colleague, who had also worked at Darlington Hall, on a housekeeping position. In her letter, she describes her married life to Stevens, who suspects she must be feeling rather unhappy. Because the Darlington residence was in great need of staff, the butler decides to pay Miss Kenton a visit, strongly encouraged by Mr. Farraday, the new owner, a somewhat extravagant American. After borrowing his new master’s car, Stevens goes to Cornwall, where his former colleague now lived.
On the way to the former housekeeper’s house, the protagonist starts pondering his impecable behaviour and attitude towards Lord Darlington. He remembers how his former master used to organize luxurious meetings between English and German aristocrats, in an effort to influente international relations in the few years before the Second World War.

Second, he reflects upon the true meaning of “dignity”, a principle he had internalized due to his father, now deceased, a rather inflexible man who had also dedicated his life to serving others. He thinks a lot about his former master’s reputation and personality, wondering whether the years and loyalty he’d invested in him had really been worth it.

Third, he starts analyzing the nature of his relationship with Miss Kenton, whom he had always kept at arm’s length, out of fear. He had been afraid to distance himself from the way he had always been told he should be, and a relationship with his colleague would surely not have been a “dignified” choice. Nonetheless, Miss Kenton had tried to get closer to him a few times, but he remained unshakable and never gave into the way he felt about her.

As the novel approaches its end, we witness the meeting between the two, as Stevens listens to Miss Kenton (now called Miss Benn, after her husband) talking about her life as a mother and soon-to-be grandmother. She doesn’t seem quite sure about having made the right choice by marrying her now husband. However, she tells Stevens she had grown to care for him. 
When Stevens tells all this to a stranger he’d started talking to, his conversation partner emphasizes the importance of living in the moment. He urges the protagonist to stop living in his past and start cherrishing the present time, as it is all we ever truly have.

To conclude, “The Remains of the Day”, by Kazuo Ishiguro, is an exceptional novel, remaining, to this day, one of the most famous novels in British literature. In 1993, a film adaptation of the book was released. It starred Emma Thompson and Sir Anthony Hopkins, and it impressively scored eight Academy Awards nominations.
