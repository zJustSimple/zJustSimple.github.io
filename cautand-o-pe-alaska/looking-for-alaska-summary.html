Summary

The novel Looking for Alaska by John Green begins when sixteen-year-old Miles Halter transfers to Culver Creek boarding school because he wants to find his Great Perhaps. At his old school he was an outsider. His hobby is learning famous last words from famous people. At Culver Creek he quickly becomes friends with his roommate Chip Martin, who everyone calls “the Colonel”. From him Miles gets the nickname "Pudge", although in reality he is very thin.

Through the Colonel, Miles meets Alaska Young. Alaska is an impulsive girl with whom Miles falls in love at first sight. During their first conversation alone, Alaska tells him about the last sentence of Simón Bolívar, asking “how he will ever get out of this labyrinth”. Alaska ponders what this labyrinth is supposed to mean and wants the answer from Miles. Miles is fascinated by Alaska, but also overwhelmed by her temperamental nature.

The group of three is joined by the Japanese Takumi and later Lara, a pretty girl from Romania, with whom Alaska wants to set Miles up. Their secret meeting place is the "Smoker’s hole", a hidden spot by the lake on the school campus. Together they drink and smoke and plan pranks. They want to perform a pre-prank to distract from the real prank that is to follow.

The night after this pre-prank, Alaska tells the others for the first time that her mother died in front of her from an aneurysm when Alaska was eight years old. She blames herself for her mother’s death because she didn't call an ambulance. Miles realizes that this event has shaped Alaska, and now he can understand her behavior better.

On the evening of the prank, Miles gets together with Lara. He has his first sexual experience with Lara, in the form of a blow job. Two days later, however, only the Colonel, Alaska, and Miles toast the successful prank in Alaska's room. The Colonel and Alaska are very drunk and suddenly Alaska suggests playing Truth or Dare. She asks Miles to kiss her as the first task, and eventually they make out on her bed until Alaska falls asleep.

In the middle of the night, however, Alaska is awakened by the phone in the hallway. When she comes back into the room after making a call, she is completely distressed. She says she needs to leave urgently and that Miles and the Colonel should help her. Miles and the Colonel are so blindsided by her emotional outburst that they distract the dean of the students (“the Eagle”) with firecrackers, as if they were playing a prank, so Alaska can drive away in her car unnoticed.

The next day, they learn that Alaska died in a fatal car accident. This comes as a great shock to them, and Miles and the Colonel blame themselves for her death, since they allowed her to drive so drunk. First alone, then together with Takumi and Lara, they try to find out what actually happened that night.

Most of all, they wonder if it was an accident or if Alaska committed suicide. They find out that Alaska was on the phone with her boyfriend that night. During the phone call she discovered a daisy painted by herself on the wall, which reminded her of her mother and that she had forgotten the anniversary of her death. That was why she was so upset when she returned to the room. She wanted to go to the cemetery and put flowers on her mother's grave. However, the question remains whether she took her own life on purpose.

In memory of Alaska, the friends perform a prank already pre-planned by her, which they call the Alaska Young Memorial Prank. With this, they can begin to say goodbye to Alaska.

When Miles learns that Takumi also encountered Alaska on the night of the accident and did not stop her either, he realizes that they must all forgive themselves. They have all made mistakes, but didn’t know better at that time. They must now accept that they will never find out if Alaska took her own life, and they forgive her as well.
