Glossary of key terms

On this page you can find words that are particularly useful to know when studying the Vietnam War topic guide. Click each word for a full definition in English.

Agent Orange (s)
 

A military herbicide widely used by the US military during the Vietnam War. Initially thought safe for human beings, it was later discovered that the chemical had serious negative effects on people’s health.

Americanization (s)
 

Refers to the increasing involvement of the United States in the Vietnam War.

ARVN (s)
 

ARVN, or the Army of the Republic of Vietnam, were the forces of the South Vietnamese Government. Also known as the South Vietnamese Army (SVA). During the Vietnam War, this army was allied with the forces of the United States.

attrition (s)
 

A reduction in an army's numbers or combat readiness - typically as a result of exhaustion, constant harassment, or attack.

Cold War (s)
 

The ideological conflict between the United States and the Soviet Union during the second half of the 20th century (1947-1991). The term ‘cold’ is used because no direct large-scale fighting took place between the two nations, particularly due to the fact that both nations had nuclear weapons. However, the conflict was characterised by proxy wars (see below), which were fought by the allied states of the United States and the Soviet Union.

colony (s)
 

A country or region under the political control of another country.

communist state (s)
 

A totalitarian system of government in which a single political party controls state-owned means of production and private property is abolished.

containment (s)
 

A geopolitical strategy to stop the expansion of an enemy. It was a popular foreign policy during the Cold War, mostly attributed to the United States and its efforts to prevent the spread of communism.

coup (s)
 

A sudden attempt by a small group of people to take over the government, usually through violent methods such as assassination. 

credibility gap (s)
 

A term that came into use in the United States during the 1960s and 1970s. It was used to describe public scepticism about President Lyndon B. Johnson’s Administration, particularly their statements about the progression of the Vietnam War.

defoliant (s)
 

A chemical spray or dust applied to plants to make them lose their leaves. It was used by the US military to prevent enemies from hiding in forests, to destroy enemy crops, and to make bombing targets more visible. Agent Orange (see above) was the most widely used defoliant during the war. 

demilitarized zone (s)
 

A neutral territory or an area in which military activities and the presence of the military are forbidden.

destroyer (s)
 

A fast, manoeuvrable long-endurance warship intended to escort and defend other vessels.

Domino theory (s)
 

A political theory that gained popularity between the 1950s and the 1980s. It was used to argue that if one country in a region adopted a communist government, then the surrounding countries would also fall under the influence of communism, in a domino-like effect. The theory was used during the Cold War to justify the intervention of the United States in international conflicts.

ecocide (s)
 

The destruction of large areas of the natural environment as a consequence of human activity.

escalation (s)
 

When something increases in strength, size or degree. When referring to a conflict such as the Vietnam War, escalation usually means that the conflict itself is intensified, or that someone's involvement in the conflict becomes greater. 

The opposite of escalation is deescalation (s).

guerrilla warfare (s)
 

An unconventional form of warfare in which a small group of fighters such as a paramilitary organisation uses unconventional military strategies to fight a bigger group such as a traditional state army. Typical guerrilla tactics include hiding among civilian populations, using detailed knowledge of areas to arrange ambushes, not wearing recognisable uniforms, and various other strategies to improve the odds against a numerically superior foe.  

ideology (s)
 

The set of ideas and beliefs of a group or political party.

Indochina (s)
 

A grouping of French colonial territories in Southeast Asia. It was comprised of territories now belonging to Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, and China.

insurgency (s)
 

A form of revolt against a government. An insurgency typically describes a revolt that is less powerful in strength and numbers than an organised revolution.

loyalist (s)
 

A person who is or remains loyal especially to a political cause, party, government, or sovereign. It refers to the part of the army that remained loyal to South Vietnamese leader Ngo Dinh Diem and helped him defeat a coup attempt during the Vietnam War.

napalm (s)
 

A thick substance that contains gasoline, used in bombs that cause a destructive fire over a wide area. Napalm bombs were used by the US military during the Vietnam War. The use of napalm became controversial as photos emerged of civilian victims (including children) who had been burned or driven away by the rain of fire.

The United Nations banned the use of napalm on civilian targets in 1980, but it is still legal to use against military targets. 

PAVN (s)
 

PAVN, or the People's Army of Vietnam, is the military force of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam. In the context of the Vietnam War, the army was the military force of North Vietnam and was supported mainly by the Soviet Union. It is also referred to as the North Vietnamese Army (NVA).

proxy war (s)
 

An armed conflict between two states which act on behalf of other parties that are not directly in open confrontation against each other. Though the US and the Soviet Union were never directly at war during the Cold War, various proxy wars were fought instead. The Vietnam War is typically regarded as a proxy war.

satellite state (s)
 

A country that is technically independent, but still under heavy political, economic, and military influence or control from another country.

Soviet Union (s)
 

The Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR), commonly known as the Soviet Union, was a socialist state that existed from 30th of December, 1922, to 26th of December, 1991. It extended from Eastern Europe to Northern Asia and included Russia and 14 other socialist republics.

Vietnamization (s)
 

A policy adopted during the Nixon administration which aimed to end American involvement in the Vietnam War while transferring the task of fighting the communists to the South Vietnamese Army.

Viet Cong (s)
 

A communist political organisation and guerrilla army based in South Vietnam that fought against the Governments of South Vietnam and the United States during the Vietnam War. The name stands for ‘Vietnamese Communist.’ It was also known as the National Liberation Front for Southern Vietnam (NLF).

Viet Minh (s)
 

A national independence coalition formed by Ho Chi Minh on 19th of May, 1941. It was an organised resistance group, seeking independence for Vietnam. The Viet Minh opposed the re-occupation of Vietnam by France. After taking control of the North Vietnamese Government, Ho Chi Minh and his supporters also opposed South Vietnam and the United States in the Vietnam War.
