Harry Potter and The Philosopher's Stone - Summary

‘Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone’ is the first of a series of seven books written by British author J.K. Rowling. After having been published first in 1997, the book still attracts impressively high numbers of readers. Its subject is highly original and it revolves around the surprising turn taken by an eleven year old boy’s destiny.

Harry Potter was an eleven-year-old boy who lived with Mr. and Mrs. Dursley at number four, Privet Drive, in the London suburbs. He had lost both his parents following a car crash (according to his aunt and uncle), at the age of one. Petunia was his mother’s sister and she used to talk about how strange Lily (Harry’s mother) had been as a child. Vernon and Petunia had a child, Dudley, a violent, not very smart, but notably far young boy who bullied Harry every chance he got. Harry wasn’t loved by his relatives, and they always gave him the impression that they were raising him out of some sort of obligation.

The little boy’s suffering comes to an end one day when a letter for him is delivered. The ‘sender’ section said: ‘Hogwarts, School of Witchcraft and Wizardry’. When they see it, Petunia and Vernon become very anxious and, when they keep reufsing to let Harry read his letter, more and more letters arive, being flown in by owls in plain daylight. In the middle of a nervous breakdown, uncle Vernon takes his wife, child and Harry to a room in a building placed on an isolated cliff, located on an island, during the night. That’s when Hagrid shows up. He was a half giant who seemed to have Harry’s best interest at heart. He brought him a birthday cake (first real birthday present he’d ever gotten) and told him the truth about himself. Turns out, Harry was a wizard, and so had his parents been, before they died at the hands of the darkest wizard in the world: Voldemort. When Voldemort tried to kill Harry, something inexplicable happened, that left the boy with nothing but a lightning bolt shaped scar on his forehead. In turn, Voldemort had been reduced to a strange, incomplete form of life, who, deprived of a body, was forced to inhabit other living beings in order to survive. For the moment, he was gone, but no one knew for how long.

Hagrid takes Harry to Diagon Alley, where the boy gets to buy school supplies and start getting used to the wizards’ world, carefully hidden from the superficial eyes of ‘muggles’ (non-magic people). He buys his own magic wand, cauldron and Hagrid gifts him a white owl they’ll call Hedwig. Harry was now ready to go to school, and for that he had to embark on the Hogwarts express, where he meets his future best friends: Ronald Weasley and Hermione Granger.

The Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry is a quirky place and does not resemble a regular school much. There are four different houses: Gryffindoor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw and Slytherin. The headmaster, Albus Dumbledore, was the most powerful wizard alive and the only one Voldemort feared. He was a defendant of tolerance and acceptance, as opposed to his arch nemesis, whose main goal was ridding the wizardring world of ‘mudbloods’ (derogatory term used to describe people coming from non-magic families).

Volemort manages to infiltrate into Hogwarts by using one of his servants’ body as a vessel. It was professor Quirrel, who would help him kill Harry and obtain something very precious, especially given his poor physical condition: the philosopher’s stone. According to alchemists, this stone was capable of transforming any metal into gold and had resurrecting powers, granting its owner immortality.

At the end of the school year, a frightening confrontation takes place between Harry and Voldemort, where the boy finds out who his professor really was. Because the stone had been charmed to appear into the pocket of those who were not interested in using it, but purely obtaining it, Harry is the one who can suddenly feel the stone inside of one of his pockets. He manages to escape Voldemort, keep the stone (which will later on be destructed) and finish the school year honorably. All of it took place with Ron and Hermione’s help, who would be there for Harry throughout each and every difficult time he would be facing.

‘Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone’ is a wonderful introduction into the magical universe of J.K. Rowling, who has managed to charm both children and adults with her writing. Rowling’s ‘Harry Potter’ works have also been materialized into movies, video games and even theatre (written in the form of an adapted script). The ‘Harry Potter’ series thus remains one of the most impressive phenomena of contemporary universal literature.
