Essay about autumn

Dacă ai ca temă la limba engleză redactarea unui eseu despre toamnă, Essay about autumn este exemplul de care ai nevoie pentru inspirație. Acest eseu a fost redactat de un scriitor cu experiență și prezintă părerea sa despre toamnă. 

Essay

I never enjoyed autumn. Everything just seems to degrade rapidly. The air is getting colder, the nights are getting longer and rainy days just keep on coming. When I was a kid I had yet one more big reason to hate autumn: school started. Yes, I know, I may sound too pessimistic, but that’s the way I feel about this season. It doesn’t suit me at all. The only good thing that I can think of is that Halloween and Christmas are approaching.

Maybe it’s just the fact that this season makes me melancholic. I can’t stand seeing leaves fall from the trees and everything decaying around me, exactly the opposite of spring and summer. But then again, it’s the season when seeds are being planted, when grapes are being picked from their vines and turned into wine, it’s the perfect time to collect all of the fruits and vegetables that have developed during summer. All this decay will be over eventually and we’ll be ready to witness yet another uprising of green.

As humans, we fear death, but nature does not. Nature can be reborn after death, unlike us. And as much as I hate this season, I have to admit that no artist I know of ever painted death with such vibrant tints of yellow as nature does with the trees’ leaves. Perhaps light and darkness or life and death aren’t necessarily opposites. We think too much in black and white and sometimes forget that there are numerous shades in the grey area. We always want to go from A to Z really fast, not taking into consideration that there are 24 letters in between these two.

The more I think about it, autumn does have some poetic beauty attached to all the blight. It is truly paradoxical that these opposites do not negate each other, but rather they coexist in perfect harmony. In no other real world scenario could I think of such a mind blowing thing. Actually, there is one that just came to mind. When fearing the dark, it is natural to demand light at every step. But what can we do other than just cast artificial light during the night, whilst the darkness keeps growing ever so terrifying? Nature needs both light and darkness in order to function correctly.

My mind must’ve wondered a little whilst writing this, as I am not usually this emotional and philosophical over such an ordinary subject as a season. Or maybe it’s just the melancholy that gets a grip on me while I look out the window and see grey skies, rain and leaves falling off trees endlessly. When adding all these things up, though, I certainly cannot feel as sad as I usually felt during this time period.

Although I still prefer summer, the ideas that I just discussed earlier made me realize that autumn isn’t half as bad as I initially thought. Maybe humans tend to be too judgemental at times and forget that beyond looks there are more important things in life. I now anxiously await Christmas, New Year’s Eve and spring to finally come in all its glory.

Essay about the winter season

Essay about the winter season este o lucrare care ne amintește de cel mai răcoros dintre anotimpuri, iarna. Deși este o perioadă rece a anului, în care noaptea este predominantă, Essay about the winter season ne arată acest anotimp prin ochii unui copil, amintindu-ne de vremea în care frigul și întunericul treceau neobservate, iar zăpada și Moș Crăciun erau vedetele de sezon. 

Essays

Winter is the coldest season throughout the year. I personally prefer summer, but I love winter because that is when Christmas and the New Year celebration start. In fact, when I was a kid, I looked forward to the winter season exactly for this reason. I love when it’s snowing on Christmas just as much as I loved it back in the good old days.

I think that, as kids, we all loved winter because it would be the perfect time to get the sleigh out of the shed and just play in the snow all day long. We used to make snowmen and throw snowballs at each other. Sure, as teenagers, we might also do those things from time to time, but it felt way better when we were kids. The only thing that used to bother me as a child regarding winter was the unforgiving cold. Nowadays, when I see snow any other time of the year apart from Christmas, I feel like doing nothing but stay at home and drink hot cocoa.

Something tells me that when I’ll get even older I’ll start hating winter even more. I see a lot of people who go to work that keep complaining about the short days and long nights, about how it’s dark both when they go to and leave work. I also find this aspect about winter appalling. Technically, the day hasn’t passed, if you look at the clock, but in practical terms, by 5 PM, even on daylight savings time, it’s pitch dark outside. It’s no wonder that some people get grumpy during this season. I think we all start to miss childhood in this time of the year.

On the flipside, for us students, the school program is much shorter. All classes are reduced by 5 minutes, as well as the breaks, so that we may get home an hour earlier, which is a huge advantage for the people who don’t live within the city in which they attend school. Even so, they still get home harder because cars and train need to travel slower in order to avoid accidents caused by slipping on ice. In fact, slipping is a major problem even on foot, so it’s harder getting home even for those who live near the school.

Even so, winter still gives me a unique feeling. It makes me wonder about how nature is reborn every year. Although everything around us seems dead, trees lose all their leaves and the sun seems to be far away, after winter passes, nature grows into fruition once more. It makes you think about the circle of life and about how the old needs to pass so that the new can shine as bright as ever. Winter makes us appreciate the beauty of summer even more. If there was only a single season throughout the entire year, as in some areas of the globe, we wouldn’t appreciate its beauty as much as we do.

Whether you like it or not, winter does have its benefits. Even if you despise winter, you still look forward for the holidays, Christmas and New Year’s Eve. We should seek the good in everything and we shouldn’t let ourselves get sad because of the cold weather and lack of heat or sun. Cherish winter, because soon nature will rise once more.

Essay about Christmas

Orice perioadă a anului ar fi, Crăciunul a fost și va fi una dintre sărbătorile preferate ale tuturor. Essay about Christmas surprinde această sărbătoare, arătând câteva dintre caracteristicile inconfundabile de care oamenii se bucură de la mic la mare. Fie că e vorba de colinde, cadouri sau Moș Crăciun, Essay about Christmas ne amintește de spiritul sărbătorilor. Essay about Christmas este o lucrare excelentă care scoate în evidență trăsăturile unui eseu cu caracter personal și încurajeaza cititorul să empatizeze cu descrierile prezentate.

Essay

Christmas is a time of celebration for children and Christians alike. At its origin, the holiday is about the birth of Jesus Christ, but it has garnered a few more traditions since then. What’s the first thing that comes to your mind when you think about Christmas? Your answer will be “presents”, most likely. 

Who could forget the childhood Christmas mornings? Who could forget being excited on Christmas Eve about Santa Claus's arrival? What child wouldn’t have wanted to go out caroling with his friends during those days? The sense of wonder, that tender innocence, the playful joy, that is how I would describe that time period during childhood years.

So, how exactly do people celebrate Christmas? Well, there are a few key elements to this holiday, apart from the birth of Jesus or the coming of Santa Claus. The first thing that comes to mind is presents. It’s not just Saint Nick who delivers presents. People also have the custom of gifting each other something, be it symbolical or something with financial value. Also, what would be Christmas without the good old carols? Listening to songs related to this holiday is a huge part of its charm. Who hasn’t heard “Jingle Bells” or “Silent Night” and instantly fell in love with them?

Who could forget all the season specific food and delicious treats? Your taste buds will be delighted during this time of the year, as you will come across all sorts of things to eat. Let’s not forget about the tree decoration. Every year, around this holiday, people decorate their Christmas tree in order to lighten up the atmosphere in their homes. The lights, the globes, the tinsel, the candy canes and the star, are all used in tandem to give life to the beautiful tree.

Symbolically, Christmas is about being a better person, about helping others and spreading joy everywhere you can. 

It is also a time meant to be spent with friends and family. Usually, people who go to work or to university at a long distance, come home for this holiday in order to do just that. To me, Christmas holds a special place in my heart, and every time I am home during this season, when I see the snow, smell the freshly cut oranges and hear those classic tunes, I get reminded of my sweet childhood. I get reminded of how I used to go sleigh riding as a kid, about how I used to go caroling, as many other children do today.

Sometimes I wish I could turn back time just to experience that feeling one last time. But, then again, being an accomplished student with spare time is good enough, especially on Christmas.

As a personal note, I would like to remind everyone to never stop feeling like a child on this holiday, for it is certainly the best feeling in the world. Even as I am writing this, I anxiously await the arrival of Santa Claus and spending more time my family once again. Christmas should be every day.
