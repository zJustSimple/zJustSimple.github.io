Essay about family

Familia este unul dintre cei mai importanți factori care ne formează ca persoane, iar Essay about family este aici ca să dovedească asta. Începând de la primii pași și cei 7 ani de acasă, până la sfaturi de viață, Eseul ne arată cum familia ne ajută să ne integrăm în societate și să supraviețuim într-o lume greu de înțeles. Essay about family este o lucrare încurajatoare, care ne arată cum membrii familiei ne pot fi în același timp îngrijitori, mentori dar mai ales prieteni și aliați.

Essay

“The love of family and the admiration of friends is much more important than wealth and privilege.”

From the moment we are born, we are surrounded by the people who are closest to us - family. We learn to walk and talk because of them, as they closely watch over us while we grow up. We get our basic education from them: how to act in society, how to communicate with others, what it means to be a good person and how to analyze our surroundings and avoid danger.

Whether it’s our parents, our brothers and sisters, our grandparents, our uncles and aunts or simply distant relatives, we can learn something new from each individual in our family. The family’s role in a person’s life is to assure all their basic necessities, whilst not ignoring their education.

A family should be made up of people who you can trust and count on. We cannot ignore all the news about parents beating up and abusing their children, but even in those cases, they are the people closest to the child, sadly. And their way of treating them will influence their kids when they grow up, so, in a sense, those children get an education, but in a wrong, harmful way. A family’s influence over a child is immeasurable. 

But leaving aside the negative examples, a family is the most important gift we receive. Whenever something happens in our lives, they are there to gather the joys and wipe away our sorrows. They are there to teach us everything we need to know about life, about how we need to act in society, about how we should speak to other people, about the things we need to pursue, but also about the things we need to avoid in order to prosper and become better human beings.

You can learn from your family things that you will not necessarily learn in school, such as cooking, cleaning and taking care of yourself before adulthood approaches, so that you will have time to prepare for when you will be on your own. In my opinion, that is the most important aspect in life for any human - being loved and being prepared to make good use of one’s skills and education.

Finally, there’s one more thing that family can offer you and very few other people can offer. Friendship. While people you meet during your life might be enemies disguised as friends, or simply might be indifferent to you, family will always be your friends and you know you can count on them no matter how hard your situation is and no matter what needs you may have or difficulties you may stumble upon.

In conclusion, family is the most important gift any person could ever receive. Not only are they the best friends a person can have, they are also indispensable allies in one’s education and mental health. A family is uniquely brilliant and irreplaceable. 

Essay about childhood

Te-ai jucat vreodată de-a prinselea sau de-a v-ați ascunselea? Dacă măcar unul dintre jocurile de mai sus îți sună cunoscut, Essay about childhood este o lucrare pe gustul tău. Prezentând copilăria dintr-o perspectivă simplistă și lipsită de griji, Eseul ne duce într-o perioada mai plăcută pe care majoritatea dintre noi ne-o aducem aminte cu nostalgie și o picătură de părere de rău pentru că a trecut. Essay about childhood este o lucrare care descrie pe larg perioada copilăriei, făcând comparația dintre perioada infantilă și adolescență.

Essay

“It's never too late to have a happy childhood.”

Think of the time when you were a child, when life was much simpler and worries were far away. You wanted to grow up so that people could take you seriously, but as soon as you started becoming more mature, you realized just how poetic and careless the old days were. You didn’t have to go to school, pay bills, work, worry about all sorts of problems etc. Back then, you were just an innocent person barely discovering the world.

They say childhood is the best period of one’s life, and I fully agree with that statement. Although every period of a person’s life has its own charm, nothing can quite beat the innocence and sense of wonder that you have as a kid. Friends were much easier to acquire and have fun with, hopes and dreams were in full swing, the education you got was easy to comprehend, yet so essential to life, the memories are like nothing else. Being taken care of day and night is something that we all long for, yet we only get it in this time frame.

Then comes the transition into adolescence and into adulthood, respectively. You start having more and more responsibilities, you start to worry more, sometimes even about things that only exist in your imagination, you start being scolded for things that were forgiven easily in your childhood, but you also start maturing, you start to accumulate and assimilate tons of new information that you couldn’t process as a kid.

The trade-off is pretty good, but you still feel nostalgic at times. Sure, not everyone has had a happy childhood, but it can never be too late to turn it all around. Be foolish when the opportunity lies ahead, dream about stuff, act funny with your friends, play some games even. Do anything that makes you happy and reminds you of childhood joy, for it is a period which you can never go back to, but you can always remember and cherish it.

There were lots of games that we played as kids, popular ones such as hide and seek, truth or dare, cops and robbers, duck-duck-goose and many more. We used to play outside till dawn, when our parents would call us home for dinner. We didn’t have the technology of today, but we couldn’t care less. Even today’s kids can still do all the things we did. Unlike some people think, gadgets might not be necessarily bad for a child, if attentive parents explain to their children that they shouldn’t spend too much time with said gadgets.

Teach your children to go outside and have fun, educate them about life, let them learn about things that they can learn faster as children. Do not be the type of parent who keeps their kid locked in the house all day, for that is the exact opposite of what a developing human should do. Instead, let them experiment with other people their age and find what they like to do. That way, they will turn into open minded adults who are responsible enough to eventually take care of themselves,

I think everyone should cherish their childhood and talk about it from time to time. One should not live in the past, but should also not let it be forgotten, because the past is what formed us, it is what made us who we are today. Remembering the past and building the future should be intertwined.

Essay about hometown

Acesta este un eseu care are la bază o experiență care ne-a marcat pe toți, orașul în care am copilărit. Pentru unii dintre noi orașul natal este și cel curent, pentru alții este un loc pe care și-l amintesc cu drag, iar pentru alții un loc nu tocmai plăcut. Oricare ar fi părerea ta despre orașul în care ai copilărit, Essay about hometown ne arată influența crucială pe care mediul o are asupra noastră, mai ales la o varsta fragedă. Essay about hometown este o compunere de nota 10 care încearcă să aducă un sentiment de nostalgie în inima cititorului, în același timp aducând argumente solide de ce orașul copilăriei este un loc important pentru fiecare dintre noi.

Essay

When thinking about your hometown, you think about the place where you grew up. Although a lot of us migrate to the big cities to go to a more prestigious high school or college, we never forget the place that forged us, the place that laid the foundation of our personalities and traits, the place where we used to stay when we were children – our hometown.

My hometown is a small, quiet and peaceful town called Rovinari, which is located in southwest Romania. The town is so small that basically all the people are acquaintances. Back when I was a kid, me, my classmates and my friends jokingly called it a village with apartment buildings because of the town’s size and commonality. Although lacking in career opportunities, I consider it the perfect place for adults to raise their children, simply because it offers kids the convenience of always feeling safe and socializing easily due to the small community mentality.

It may not be the perfect place to grow up as a teenager and young adult, but as a child, I don’t think many other options can beat it. Although I left this town when I started high school, I cannot deny the fact that its impact on me was immense. You know how parents today are annoyed by the fact that their children spend too much time in front of the TV or on the internet. Well, in a town like this, although doable, you will hardly ever feel the need to just stay inside, in front of the screen. All the kids were outside, riding bikes, playing different games such as hide and seek and the likes. It was hard not to join in.

Furthermore, doing these things can greatly improve your personality and charisma as a teenager. While others who lived in bigger cities were quieter and more introverted, we who lived in small towns were quite talkative and always looking to socialize with as many people as possible. We also had no notion of “bad neighbourhoods”, because in our small town everyone knew each other and they were all friendly. In the big city, you’re likely to stumble upon one or two areas that are not so keen to strangers. That, to me, is another advantage of my hometown.

Although bigger cities have a different mentality, I can say that I have adapted well to them and I wouldn’t return too soon to my hometown simply because of all the fun things that are at your disposal. But I do think that growing up in a small town is an advantage, at least when it comes to developing your personality as a child. My hometown will always be in my heart and I’ll keep it with me wherever I may go. Maybe I’ll go back there once I’ve had enough of the opportunities that the big city has to offer.

Essay about the relationship between brother and sister

Dacă nu ai frați sau surori, Essay about the relationship between brother and sister este aici să-ți descrie experiența. Încercând să descrie experiența de a avea un frate, fară a încălca limita de cuvinte a unei compuneri, Essay about the relationship between brother and sister menționează punctele principale cum ar fi gelozia dintre frați, atitudinea de mentor a fratelui mai mare, rivalitatea, prietenia sau cunoașterea reciprocă, care uneori este confundată cu telepatia.

Essay

Siblings are an important part of our family. They are like friends that we receive since the moment we first breathe the air on this earth. We might not get along with them all the time, but they are dear to us and are irreplaceable. 

The relationship between the two can vary from family to family. Usually, the younger sibling looks up to the older one as a leader and as a mentor. The older sibling is usually jealous of the younger one, as the latter receives more attention from the parents once they are born. Even so, they tend to care for one another because at the end of the day, whether they like it or not, they are bound to spend quite a few years together.

The relationship between brothers and sisters is heavily bound by each one’s personality, character, temperament and the education that they receive. Thus, we can encounter several different feelings which may occur between them, such as friendship, resentment, hate, rivalry, or even a sort of telepathy between them, because growing up together means that they have enough time to develop certain linguistic codes between them, be it verbal or nonverbal.

It is advised that if there are siblings of different genders in a family each should receive a different room, for reasons that should be obvious. Everyone likes their privacy and kids are no exception. If they stay together in the same room, then they will inevitably fight for space where to put their distinct set of toys and so on. Siblings also have a few not-so-noticeable effects on each other, if they are the opposite sex. Usually, boys and girls both seek partners that reminds them to a certain extent of their opposite-gender sibling and opposite-gender parent.

When they get older, they can also learn from one another how both genders interact and type of discussions they have so that they can better understand others. Puberty is usually a very awkward period, but having a sibling of the opposite sex can make things easier due to the extra knowledge gained from the other side. It might seem unimportant at first, but this helps out people in the long run, especially when they are adults who need to interact with all sorts of people on different occasions. 

We might not be all blessed with a sibling, but we can make friends that can have a similar role. Even then, the experience of having a sibling is unbeatable, because no one knows us better than them.
