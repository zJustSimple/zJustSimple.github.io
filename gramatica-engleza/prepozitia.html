Prepoziția în limba Engleză

Prepoziția în Limba Engleză/the preposition in English reprezintă o parte de vorbire importantă, deoarece de folosirea corectă a prepozițiilor depinde stăpânirea verbelor, adjectivelor și a substantivelor. 

Motivul, prepoziția în Limba Engleză/the preposition in English nu poate îndeplini funcții sintactice în propoziție, ea însoțind, de regulă un substantiv articulat, după cum poți observa în exemplele de mai sus, dar și un pronume de obiect.

Cuprins
  • Cele mai comune prepoziții
  • Plus nouns
  • Plus verbs
  • Plus adjectives

Prepoziția este o parte neflexibilă de vorbire care indică raporturile sintactice dintre alte părți de vorbire:

I listen to the radio every morning.

John is at the office now.

The books are on the table.

Prin urmare, prepozițiile nu pot îndeplini funcții sintactice în propoziție, ele însoțind, de regulă un substantiv articulat, după cum poți observa în exemplele de mai sus, dar și un pronume de obiect.

Desigur, vor exista și excepții în care substantivul poate fi nearticulat: at home, at breakfast/lunch/dinner, at war/ sea, at school/church, by car/bus/plane, by night/day, by mistake, etc.

De obicei, aceste prepoziții sunt formate dintr-un singur cuvânt (in, at, on), dar există și prepoziții compuse din mai multe cuvinte: in the middle, with the help of, thanks/due to,by means of, etc.

 

Cele mai comune prepoziții

at - care indică locul exact în spațiu, dar și în timp: I'll see you at the cinema at 8 o'clock.

dar se folosește și cu at night/the weekend: At the weekend I like to relax.

in - care indică limitele unei zone sau poziția : I went for a walk in the park.

dar este folosit și cu lunile, anii sau secolele: I was born in (September) 1984.

anotimpurile: John likes to visit Paris in summer.

momentele principle ale zilei: I work better in the morning/afternoon/evening.

on - care indică o suprafață: The flowers are on the table.

dar și zilele săptămânii sau alte ocazii speciale: On my birthday (September 22) I like to meet with my friends.

Aici este important de menționat faptul că atunci când folosim next, last, every sau this împreună cu zilele săptămânii, nu este necesară adăugarea prepoziției on:

See you next Monday!

Every Saturday John plays tennis.

to -  este prepoziția care indică direcția și/sau mișcarea: John is going to the cinema/bank/office/school.

 

Prepoziția by indică agentul, după cum am amintit în capitolul dedicat vorbirii indirecte (The house was built by my grandfather.), dar și instrumentul, împreună cu pepoziția with: John goes to work by bus.

I opened the door with the key.

 

For este prepoziția care, în mod obișnuit, indică scopul, dar și durata:

Let’s go for a walk/ swim.

I made this for you.

I've been waiting for you for an hour.

 

From indică originea sau începutul:

John is from London.

From the beginning, I thought this project was easy.

I work from Monday to Friday.

Alte prepoziții des folosite sunt: above, after, against, along, among, before, behind, between, below, beneath, beside, close to, down, in front of, into, near, next to, off, onto, opposite, outside, over, past, round, through, throughout, towards, under, underneath, up.

Deși nu există reguli fixe pentru utilizarea acestor prepoziții, trebuie totuși să fii atent la anumite diferențe subtile de sens, ca și în următoarele cazuri:

beside/ besides

John usually sits beside me in class. (=next to me.)

Besides us, there were many other people at the event. (=In addition to us)

between/ among

Between you and me, the test was easy. (=only the two of us.)

John was among the people who attended the meeting. (=there were other people at the meeting, and John was one of them.)

like/as

Mary acts like a princess in distress all the time. (=She is not really a princess, but she acts as if she were.)

John works as an engineer. (=that is his capacity.)

off/of

I don't know the answer off the top of my head. (=I don't know it right away, I must think about it. Also, part of the idiom.)

The cake is made of chocolate. (=chocolate is the ingredient out of which the cake is made.)

Plus nouns

Există cazuri în limba engleză când anumite substantive trebuie să fie însoțite în mod obligatoriu de o prepoziție.

Mai jos, le enumerăm pe cele mai importante.

at

a profit/loss/ cost - I was at a loss for words when I heard the news.

your convenience - You can contact me at your earliest convenience.

in

writing/ advance - You may express your interest in this position in writing.

my opinion - In my opinion, you are wrong.

debt - That company is in debt.

stock - We may have this shirt in stock.

a magazine - I read about this in a magazine.

increase/decrease in - Lately, there has been an increase in sales.

for

for a reason/need/demand/order/request/application for something - I have a reason for saying this.

for sale - This house is for sale.

for lunch/breakfast/dinner - I always have cereal for breakfast.

of

the cause of - Heavy rains are the cause of the flood.

advantage/ disadvantage/ characteristic of - One advantage of this approach is gaining more customers.

on

on vacation/ holiday/ business/ a trip - John will be on vacation for the next week.

on sale - This computer is on sale. We can buy it now.

on hold - I was put on hold when I tried to contact John.

on the phone/ TV/ the radio/ internet - I heard the news on the radio.

on time - Luckily, I got to work on time this morning.

out

out of order/date - This elevator is out of order; we must use the stairs.

to

to my mind - To my mind, this is the correct solution.

access/ attitude/ reaction/solution/invitation to - John's reaction to this problem was not good.

with

connection/ contact/ relationship with - John has a good relationship with his co-workers.Verbs

Plus verbs

În ceea ce privește „verbs with prepositions in English”, deoarece o prepoziție cere un complement, doar verbele tranzitive pot fi urmate de acestea.

Este foarte important, pe de-o parte, să facem distincția între verbele prepoziționale și verbele cu particulă adverbială (phrasal verbs).

Astfel, prepositional verbs = verb + preposition: believe in,cope with, deal with, depend on, look at, look for, listen to, see through, think about, talk about, wait for sunt doar câteva dintre verbele prepoziționale cu care ești, cu siguranță, familiarizat.

Ele se deosebesc de phrasal verbs într-aceea că structura lor este fixă și vor fi mereu urmate de un complement direct:

  • I don't want to listen to this radio station anymore.
  • Finishing the project depends on all of us.
  • I've been waiting (for you) for an hour.

 

Phrasal verbs, pe de altă parte,  = verb + adverb (+preposition).

De cele mai multe ori aceste particule adverbiale schimbă întru totul sensul verbelor pe care le însoțesc:

 

  • John always sees Mary off at the airport. (=He doesn't literally see her at the airport, but he accompanies her to the airport whenever she leaves.)
  • I'll pick you up on my way home. (=You haven't fallen, so I won't literally pick you up. Rather, I'll give you a ride in my car.)
  • John really looks up to his father. (=John is not looking up at his tall father. Rather, he admires him.)
  • Mary has fair curly hair; she has taken after her mother. (= Mary has not taken her mother's hair. Rather, she inherited that trait from her mother who has the same fair curly hair.)
  • Global warming has brought about many climate changes. (=Global warming has not taken climate changes by the hand and brought them about. Rather, it was the cause for these changes.)

 

În cele ce urmează, îți prezentăm o listă comună cu cele mai des folosite verbe prepoziționale și cu particulă adverbială.  

Este important de reținut că acest tip de verbe pot avea mai multe sensuri, după cum vei putea vedea în continuare.

Te îndemnăm să cauți sensul acestor propoziții daca nu cumva îl poți extrage din context, pentru a asigura o înțelegere adecvată a acestui capitol:

 

ask

about - John asked about Mary's family.

around - I don't know who is responsible for this, but I'll ask around.

for - Ask John for help.

in - Ask your friends in.

out - John asked Mary out (on a date).

over - I asked our neighbours over for dinner.

believe

in someone/ something - I believe in this project. but I believe that you are right.

break

into - The burglars broke into the office building.

down - The car engine broke down, so my car is in the garage.

in - It’s always a good idea to break your new shoes in in order to avoid hurting your feet.

off - You can break off a piece of that chocolate if you want

       John and Mary broke off their engagement.

out - The conflict broke out in April.

       Because of my allergy to cats, I broke out in a rash.

up - The glass broke up into little pieces when it hit the floor.

            I didn't understand what you said, you're breaking up.

             Mary broke up with John two times before they got engaged.

bring

about - The collaboration brought about new changes.

along - You can bring your friends along to the party.

around - It took John a long time, but he finally managed to bring me around to his perspective.

           Mary brought some cake around when she visited me yesterday.

back - Visiting this city brought back a lot of good memories.

           I forgot to bring my computer back home.

down - The low number of votes brought down the party.

forth - The research brought forth a lot of compelling evidence.

on - The author will bring out a new book in summer.

up - Now that you bring it up, we did discuss this during the meeting.

call

back - Please tell John to call me back when he finishes his break.

in - The sink is not working, so I must call in a plumber to fix it.

off - The event was called off because of the heavy rain.

on - Since we were in the neighbourhood, we decided to call on Mary.

check

in - We checked in at the airport before the flight.

off - We checked the names of the students off the list, as they entered the exam room.

out - John checked out of the hotel and headed to the airport.  

come    

about - The changes came about in order to improve productivity.

across - I was looking for a book when I came across my old diary.

along  - You can come along on the trip if you want.

back - John has left the office and hasn't come back yet.

by - You should come by sometimes and see our new office.

down - The rain is coming down hard today!

forth - Anyone who witnessed the accident is asked to come forth.

in - Please, do come in! You don't need to wait for John outside.

into - Mary has recently come into some money, which is why she is planning that trip around the world.

off - The new phone came off into pieces in my hand.

out - A new book by this author will come out this autumn. We had planned to stay indoors, but the sun came out so we went to the park.

round - John didn't agree with us at first, but he came round to our way of thinking.

through - Your message hasn’t come through in my inbox yet. Mary promised to finish the project on time, and she came through.

to - John came to an hour after his surgery. The argument between the two men came to blows, so the police was called.

up - Something came up at work, so I’ll be home late.

up with - Can anyone come up with a solution to this problem?

do

away with - Their office will soon do away with the old coffee filter.

in - The writer disliked that female character, so he did her in.

up - We had our kitchen done up before we moved into the new flat.

with - I can surely do with a cold lemonade now! It’s too hot!

without - There’s no flour left, you’ll have to do without.

fall

apart - The watch fell apart in my hands.

back on - It is always good to have some money saved to fall back on in case of emergency.

behind - John was ill for a week so he fell behind on his work.

for - The jury didn’t fall for his story, so they convicted him. He fell for Mary as soon as he met her.

in - The ceiling fell in, but luckily nobody was hurt.

off - The sales fell off after the merger.

out - John and Mary fell out over that decision and they don't speak to each other at the moment.

over - Mary slipped on the floor and fell over.

through - Despite the negotiations, the deal fell through.

under - In spite of being independent, the company soon fell under his influence.

give

away - Mary’s father gave her away on her wedding day. I had no clue about the surprise party, nobody gave anything away. This month’s issue of the magazine gives away a DVD.

back - I borrowed a book from the library, but I forgot to give it back.

in - I begged John to help me with my project, until he finally gave in.

in to - The police said they will not give in to the criminal’s demands.

off - The cake was almost ready, giving off a sweet cinnamon smell. Mary gave off an air of superior elegance, as always.  

out - The leaflets were given out by the teenagers.

over - Leadership of the team was given over to John.

up - She gave up sugar in order to lose weight. John has given up trying to help his neighbours. I don't know the answer, I give up!

up on - I gave up on the idea that I might finish this project.

look

after - I always ask the neighbours to look after the dog when I'm not home.

back - Looking back, I am sure I made the right decision.

down on - She always looks down on her colleagues and thinks she is better than them.

for - I'm still looking for my sunglasses; I haven't found them yet.

forward to - I look forward to starting my vacation.

into - I don't know the answer, but I will look into it.

on - People looked on as the mugging was taking place.

out - Look out! Don't drop that vase!

out for - He advised me to look out for snakes while in the forest. John always looks out for his little sister.

over - I'll look over your homework when you finish, to make sure you got it right. When I looked over at Mary, she was drawing.

through - John will look through the article and give you his opinion.

to - Children always look to their parents for help.

up - I’ll look up the new word in the dictionary.

up to - John looks up to his father.

make

away with - The thieves made away with the woman's bag.

do with - I don't have all the ingredients for the cake, but I will make do with whatever I can find.

for - The low quality of the product made for a dramatic decrease in sales.

into - She decided to make her gold ringo into a nice pendant.

of - What do you make of the news?

off with - The burglars made off with the painting.

out - I cannot make out what she's trying to say, do you? He made out that he was ill so that he didn't have to take the test. Of course, it was a lie.  

over - Our living room was made over completely and now looks amazing.

up - After their fallout, John and Mary made up quickly. He made up a story to explain his being late, when he actually overslept.

up for - I'll make up for lost time with my friends this summer.

up to -  I'll make it up to you for my horrible behaviour.

put

away - Put those toys away when you finished playing with them. The police put him away for heft.

by - I'm trying to put some money by every month, for rainy days.

down - The police told the man to put down his gun.     

down to - The failure can be put down to his inexperience in the matter.  

in - I put in a lot of effort in this project.

in for - He put in for a transfer to another department.

off - The event was put off due to the heavy rain.

on - Put on your jacket, it's chilly outside. She has put on a lot of weight since she quit smoking.

out - Put out that cigarette! You can't smoke here.             

up - I'll put you up when you visit me, I have an extra bedroom. John wasn't going to let someone else get the credit he deserved, so he put up a fight.

up with - I cannot put up with the neighbours’ loud music all day long!

run

across/into - I ran into an old colleague at the restaurant.

after - I dislike running after the bus.

against- John ran against many great candidates for that position.

around - I'm exhausted, I've been running around all day.

into - I ran into Mary on the way home.

on - Cars run on gas.

out of - We've run out of ink, I’ll have to buy a new cartridge.

over - Could you run us over the details of the plan?

to - Whenever he needs help, he runs to his parents.

up against - They ran up against many problems before they finally opened their restaurant.

show

(a)round - Welcome to my new flat, let me show you around!

in - She showed me in to speak to the manager.

off - He is out, showing off his brand new car. That colour really shows off your green eyes.  

out  - The secretary showed him out after the interview.

through - His anger showed through, despite his attempts to look calm.

up - Not many people showed up at the event. The decrease in sales showed up in the records.

talk

at - He’s always trying to convince us of things; he'll talk at us until we give in.

back - John got expelled from the class for talking back at the Maths teacher.

down to - She always talks down on her colleagues because she thinks she is better than them.

into - I cannot believe you talked me into buying that huge pizza!

out -  They decided to meet to talk out how they can resolve the issue.

out of - I'm happy you talked me out of buying that huge pizza.

over - They talked over their differences, but they didn't manage to sort them out.

through - Talk me through the basic rules, so that I can understand better.

up - The Democratic Party is trying to talk up the effectiveness of their policies.

 

Desigur, acestea sunt doar câteva exemple des întâlnite, dar ele nu se limitează doar la prepozițiile și nici la înțelesurile prezentate, astfel că lista poate continua.

Totuși, te sfătuim să nu folosești phrasal verbs sau prepositional verbs de al căror înțeles nu ești sigur; vei risca să pari nepregătit, contrar scopului acestui ghid de gramatică. Pe de altă parte, nu este necesar să înveți multe dintre aceste verbe frazale; decide o listă de câteva din diferite categorii pe care să le domini bine și pe care să le folosești în mod adecvat.

Plus adjectives

În ceea ce privește folosirea corectă a gramaticii limbii engleze, numite adjective/adjective vor fi și urmate obligatoriu de o prepoziție/preposition. 

Pentru a te ajuta să înțelegi acest aspect, profesorii noștri de limba engleză îți pun la dispoziție o listă cu cele mai populare „adjectives plus prepositions”. 

 

Adjective - preposition list

clever/ kind/ generous/ good/ intelligent/ nice/ polite/ stupid/ silly + of

It was kind of you to help me with the bags.

Totuși, atunci când ne referim la comportamentul față de altcineva (direct object), aceste adjective vor fi urmate de prepoziția to: Thank you for being nice to me!

upset/ angry/furious about something, with someone, for something

I am very upset with you for not calling me back.

John is furious about not being invited to the event.

pleased/ satisfied/ bored/ fed up + with

I am very satisfied with the result.

John is fed up with his chores.

surprised/shocked/mesmerised/ amazed/ astonished at/ by something

Everyone was shocked at/by the news.

afraid/scared/ frightened/terrified of something

I am terrified of snakes.

to be married to someone, una din cele mai des întâlnite greșeli în limba engleză

John is married to Mary.

famous/ known for

John is famous for his tennis skills.

interested in

I am not interested in playing tennis.

good/bad/excellent at something

John is really good at tennis.
