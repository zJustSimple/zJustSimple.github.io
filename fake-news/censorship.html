Censorship and fake news

In countries that guarantee free speech policies, it is difficult to keep fake news under control. Because of this impediment, some have suggested that increased censorship can help prevent the spread of fake news. However, increased censorship might itself present a different problem.

The measure of increased censorship can compromise the freedom of the press, especially if used in less democratic states. Therefore, the governments in those countries could limit public access to news, regardless of whether it is fake or real, or suppress the real news in favour of fake news that favours the government’s agenda. By laying constraints on mainstream media through measures like censorship and restrictive legislation, states can more easily ensure the circulation of government-backed disinformation and propaganda.

It is also important to note what a government considers as fake news. Donald Trump is notorious for referring to real news that criticises his policies as “fake news”. Therefore, by using his position of authority, Trump discredits legitimate news sources and attempts to paint himself as the only credible source. This is a method of controlling discourse used especially in post-truth politics. Similarly, governments can also persuade Internet companies and online platforms to remove content or accounts that actively oppose their agenda. The removal of content that is not illegal can be considered a form of soft censorship, and it can be viewed as a potential threat to the freedom of speech.

China is an example of a non-democratic state where the government exerts extensive control over all media capable of reaching a wide audience. As a result, news goes through a censorship process regardless of its truthfulness or accuracy. This method has contained the spread of fake news, limiting the negative effects it can produce. On the other hand, it also filters and gravely limits the information made available for the population.

Media professionals found out that Chinese censors are more tolerant of criticism of the government’s performance than towards news articles that challenge the government’s leading role. Furthermore, in early 2018, it became public knowledge that China debated a partnership with Google for the creation of a censor-friendly search engine, where certain terms would be omitted from the search results without notifying the user. Google received heavy criticism for their potential involvement in this project. 

Textual perspective - "Freedom of Speech and the Right to Offend" by Brendan O'Neil
 

Brendan O’Neil’s intention with the speech is to persuade the audience of the necessity of preserving the right to offend as an instrument of free speech. He also intends to criticise recent developments in universities which tend to hinder freedom of speech and the right to offend.
