The Outsiders - Book analysis

S.E. Hinton’s novel “The Outsiders” brings up a whole new interpretation to the subject Gang rivalry with her powerful story. Portrayed in an extremely realist point of view the books succeeds to not only interest people of all ages, but also create a new way of writing. The story is told through the eyes of Ponyboy, a teenage boy who lives in a world filled with both love and insecurity, never knowing when he is going to die or not. A world where a gang is the closest most loving family you could get, and a world where fight is as normal as anything else. 

The reason why I chose this book is because I can really connect with all of the Greasers. They live in the same kind of circumstances as the people from “my” town and as many other all across the world. I think that the ability to make people recognise themselves is the reason why this book is so famous. That and the book’s relevancy.

In the book there are two different gangs. The Greasers and their nemesis’ the Socials. The first and perhaps biggest difference between the two different gangs is the money issue. The Socs who came from the West side were richer than the Greasers from the East end. The Greasers were often portrayed as law abiding, long haired, dirty citizen whilst the Socs on the other hand were benefitted with better personal appearance, fancier cars and money. Even though the Greasers were looked at as the poorer low class citizens they had something special between them. They had a connection to each other, a brotherhood which made them see each other as family. When anything goes rough, the Greasers uses each other as a means of support and keeps their door opened if anything would happen to one another. They would stay with each other through thick and thin no matter what. And that is one of the main reasons why I feel like S.E. Hinton did not intentionally portray one gang more favorably. Even though the Socs are richer they do not have the same connections as the Greasers. Both gangs are imperfect and lacking. In my eyes I see that more similarities than differences. Both gangs part take in brawls, both drink and both commit crimes. The only thing making them different is the possession of money that the Socs obtains, which also is the thing that puts the Socs on a higher pedestal. The lack of money plus all the other factors I mentioned interprets the Greasers as dangerous and wild individuals.

There are many reasons why this book is still relevant and one of them being that there are still gangs in today’s society. Me for example. I grew up in a suburb where gunshots are as normal as fireworks and brawls starts before breakfast and I am well familiar with gangs. We have gangs in our suburb, whom mostly walk with the title “The older ones”. The gang are like a mixture of Socs and Greaser as they have that brotherhood but not necessarily the poverty. I even have my gang which are made of friends who I would easily call brothers. As a teenager living in these circumstances this book has really spoken to me. I can see myself in these type of situations and for that reason I have really connected with this book. I know that there will always be gangs in this world and that the amount of adolescents who lives in these kind of circumstances is baffling. The book is showing us that even though we live like that doesn’t mean that we will not accomplish something in our lifetime and that is a very strong message for me.

The other reason why I think book is so relevant is because there will always be outsiders. In this world there are too much of judging others by their appearance, the appliance of stereotypes is large and that is a thing that the book shows us not to do. In the book the Greasers and Socs see each other as rivals instead of persons. Ponyboy for example stopped seeing Randy as a Soc and started seeing him as a teenager who was tired of brawls, a teenager who had ambitions. And I think that’s a big message Hinton is trying to mediate. Another example of this comes to play when the church filled with children started to burn. Dallas, Pony and Johnny who was just ordinary Greasers valued the children’s life more and ran in to the rescue. This shows that it should not be the stereotypes that judges different gangs, it should be the individual actions. The greasers being poor and considered as animals proved their bravery and morals when they risk their lives to save people they never met. This shows us that not to judge people. Another thing I have in mind is the scenario where Cherry and Ponyboy talks about the sunsets, they remind each other that the sky is visible on the other side and at the end of the day they both watch the same sun. They are not that different. And this shows that it does not matter if you are skinny, fat, nerdy, or ugly. We all watch the same sunsets and we are all humans. We are all equal and we should not make the society think that we are not. And this is a very strong message that Hinton showed and that is one of the reasons why the book always will be relevant and immortal. Nobody should be an outsider, everyone is a human.

As a conclusion I think that Hinton did an incredible job with this book. The realism makes the book connectable because it brings up scenarios that still to this day can occur. It also shows us that we can have an unrelated family and still love them the most. And lastly we have seen the similarities between the two gangs and learnt that we should never base our interpretations on stereotypes because at the end of the day, we are all humans.
