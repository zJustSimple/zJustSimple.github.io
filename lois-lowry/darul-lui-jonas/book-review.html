Book review

I have read the book “The giver”, written by Lois Lowry. Lois is an American author that was born 1937 in Hawaii. The Giver is her most known novel but she has also written books such as “A summer to die”, “Gathering Blue”, “Number the Stars”, etc. In 1994 Lowry won “The Newbery Medal” with “The Giver” and besides that she has won serveral more awards for her books. “The Giver” was also nominated for “Booklist Editors' Choice” and “A School Library Journal Best Book of the Year”.

To be honest I first choose this book because it was quite short and I thought it would be easy to read. But when I asked friends that I knew had read the book, and they told me that this book was interesting and fantastic, I started to read with interest. 

The story is about a boy, Jonas. Jonas lives in a world where no one stands out, no one is better than the other and where everyone sees in black and white. It is a society where no one can love or hate or have dreams or goals, everything is quite the same, all the time. When the kids in Jonas’ society turn 12, they automatically allocate a job based on their personality, they don´t get to choose what they want to do. The job that Jonas allocates is very different from the others. He is the new “receiver of memory” for his community. Jonas gets the opportunity to learn about feelings and impressions that he never knew existed. 

Jonas is the main character in this book. He is an endearing boy with a big heart. He is intelligent and has strange powers of perception that he himself does not seem to understand. His training with the old giver of memory makes him extremely passionate about the world around him and the welfare of the people he loves. I like Jonas because of his sensitivity and the way he cares about his loved ones. 

“The Giver” is also one of the main characters in this book. He is an old man that has held the community´s collective memory for many years. Now that Jonas has entered the role as the new holder of memory, “The Giver” is training him to be the best receiver of all times. “The Giver” is a genuine and kind man with a lot of experiences to share and for others to learn from.

The last main character I am describing for you is Jonas’ mother. She is a practical and pleasant woman who holds an important position at the Department of Justice. Jonas’s mother takes her work seriously, hoping to help people who break rules see the error of their ways. She frequently gives Jonas advice about the worries and fears he faces as he grows up. 

The setting is kind of important in this novel. First of all everything is in black and white, which I think is the most obvious part of how boring this society is. Otherwise, everything is very neat and “clean”. Everything is structured in order and everyone seems to behave after this. The overall picture of the setting is that is very boring and stiff. 

I think the problem Lowry is discussing in this book is that our society today is starting to look more and more like the one in this novel. In the story, people are more interested in money, work and success than in love and family, and unfortunately, our society´s becoming more like that. I think Lowry wants to warn us about this and is trying to make us understand how boring it would be. 

I admit that in the beginning I thought this book was very slow and prolix, but after around 70 pages, I began to understand what it was all about and the book captured me. I felt a little bit scared after I read the book because it felt like this was happening to me. I really liked the ending; it was well thought out and very emotional. The book gets a 3 out of 5 from me. I don not think this book belongs to a certain gender and therefore I would recommend it to everyone who likes to read.
