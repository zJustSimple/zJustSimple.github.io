Summary

“The Black Cat” by Edgar Allan Poe is written as a personal confession of a man sentenced to death. This man has been an animal lover since he was a child and has treasured the company of pets more than that of humans. The narrator marries a woman who also loves animals, and she soon brings various types of pets in their house. However, the man’s favourite pet is a black cat named Pluto.

As the years go by, the narrator starts drinking a lot and his personality changes. He becomes abusive and violent towards his pets and his wife. One night, coming home drunk, he cuts one of Pluto’s eyes out. He regrets his deed for a while, but soon he acts according to what he says to be the ingrained perverseness of his soul, and he decides to hang the cat. The narrator’s house burns down that night. All that remains of his house is a wall that bears an imprint on it that looks like a cat. Soon after that, the man starts to feel remorse for having killed the cat and looks to replace it.

One day, he comes across a very similar black cat and decides to it take home with him to replace Pluto. The cat is black but has a patch of white fur of no distinct shape on its chest. The narrator later discovers that this cat also has a missing eye, just like Pluto. Though the narrator is initially happy with the new cat in the house, he soon starts resenting its presence. The man realises that the new cat is a constant reminder of the old one and comes to associate the cat with his act of cruelty and fears the cat, especially when the cat’s patch of white fur starts to resemble the shape of a gallows.

Tormented by the cat’s presence, the man tries to kill it, but his wife stops him. Enraged, he kills his wife instead and decides to hide her body in the cellar wall. When he looks for the cat to kill it as well, he cannot find it.

In the absence of the cat, the narrator finds peace for a few days, and the detectives investigating his wife's disappearance find nothing to incriminate him. However, when the police search his house again, the narrator knocks on the cellar walls to prove their solidity. However, when he does this, a noise comes from inside the wall, making the police to tear it down and discover the dead wife’s body along with the cat, still alive next to it. The man has unintentionally trapped the cat inside the wall as well.
