Adaptations

Cuprins
  • Pride and Prejudice (1995) by BBC
  • Pride and Prejudice (2005) directed by Joe Wright
  • Lost in Austen (2008) directed by Dan Zeff

Pride and Prejudice (1995) by BBC

The six-episode British TV series adapted by Andrew Davies for BBC was a huge success. It stars Jennifer Ehle as Elizabeth Bennet and Colin Firth as Mr Darcy, and the series brought Ehle a BAFTA Award for best actress. Because it was a mini-series, and not a film, the directors and producers could include most of the characters and events from the novel, allowing for extensive character development and featuring much of Austen’s original dialogue. It is still considered one of the best adaptations and one that stays closely to the original.

The popularity of the series later resulted in Colin Firth being recast as Darcy in the 2001 film adaptation of Bridget Jones’s Diary, which borrows much of its plot and characters from Austen’s Pride and Prejudice.

Interestingly, this adaptation portrays the Bennet household as fairly well-off and picturesque. This is a clear contrast to the way the 2005 film has chosen to do it, which we outline below.

Pride and Prejudice (2005) directed by Joe Wright

One of the latest Pride and Prejudice adaptations is the 2005 film which starts Keira Knightley as Elizabeth and Matthew Macfadyen as Darcy. Being a two-hour film and not a series, it is obviously much more contracted. The director and producers have removed some of the characters and various scenes from the original novel. For example, Bingley’s sister Mrs Hurst is completely missing from the film, and Wickham also features less prominently.

The film effectively conveys the difference in wealth between the Bennets and Mr Bingley or Mr Darcy more than the BBC series mentioned above. Longbourn is depicted as being a working farm where people get dirty and handle pigs which generally makes it look much shabbier than in the BBC version, although it is still a large and relatively grand house. This obviously underlines the obstacles that especially Elizabeth and Darcy must overcome, but many Austen lovers have criticised the film for this choice since the Bennets in Austen’s novel were landed gentry.

Lost in Austen (2008) directed by Dan Zeff

This four-part British TV series is a fantasy spin-off to the original plot of Austen’s novel. In the series a young woman named Amanda Price is fed up with her ordinary life and her unromantic boyfriend when she suddenly discovers a secret portal in her flat. She then travels through time to the events in Austen’s novel of which she is a great fan and meets the charismatic Mr Darcy. Meanwhile, Elizabeth Bennet travels to the 21st century, changing the original plot dramatically.

The series was generally well received by the press. If you are looking for a postmodern adaptation of Austen’s classic, this series may be a good choice.
