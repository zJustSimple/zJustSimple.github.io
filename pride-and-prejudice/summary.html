Pride and Prejudice
  1. Engleză

Summary

Jane Austen’s novel Pride and Prejudice opens in rural England, in the village of Longbourn, where the Bennet family lives. The news that wealthy bachelor Charles Bingley has rented the Netherfield Park estate causes a lot of excitement in the village. Mrs Bennet is particularly excited as she has five unmarried daughters she wants to marry off - Jane, Elizabeth, Mary, Kitty, and Lydia.

Soon, the Bennet family attends a local ball where Mr Bingley is the guest of honour. Bingley is attracted to Jane and dances with her several times during the evening. His friend, Mr Darcy dislikes the whole event and refuses to dance with Elizabeth. His attitude makes everyone think that Darcy is arrogant and proud.

On various social occasions, Mr Darcy gets to know Elizabeth better and begins to be secretly attracted to her, while Jane and Mr Bingley also grow closer. Jane pays a social visit to Bingley at his property, but catches a cold on the way and is forced to stay in his house to recover. Elizabeth walks the long way to Mr Bingley's property in the mud to see her sister. When she arrives at the house, Charles’s sister, Miss Bingley, secretly criticises her appearance. As the two sisters stay at Netherfield Park, Mr Darcy begins to pay even more attention to Elizabeth.

When the sisters return home, they learn that the family will receive a visit from their cousin Mr Collins, a clergyman who will inherit Mr Bennet’s property because the estate is entailed so that it can only be passed on to male heirs. Not long after his arrival, Mr Collins clumsily proposes to Elizabeth, who declines his marriage offer.

During this time, the Bennet girls also meet and befriend a group of army officers who are stationed nearby. One of them, George Wickham, becomes close with Elizabeth and tells her that he grew up with Mr Darcy, but that Darcy later cheated him out of an inheritance out of envy. To Elizabeth's surprise, her friend Charlotte agrees to marry Collins.

Before winter, Darcy and Bingley leave Netherfield Park for London, and Miss Bingley writes to Jane implying that they are unlikely to return and that she hopes Mr Bingley will marry Mr Darcy’s sister Georgiana. Jane goes to stay with her aunt and uncle in London for a while, but she does not see Bingley and is treated rudely by his sister Miss Bingley, which makes Jane conclude that Mr Bingley no longer cares for her.

Come spring, Elizabeth visits Charlotte and Mr Collins at their home on the estate of Lady Catherine de Bourgh, who is also Mr Darcy’s aunt. Darcy happens to visit his aunt while Elizabeth is there and decides to declare his feelings and ask her to marry him. Elizabeth incredulously refuses his proposal, arguing that he deliberately divided Jane and Mr Bingley and that he cheated Wickham out of his inheritance, just as she does not have any feelings for him. Mr Darcy leaves in humiliation but soon afterwards gives a letter to Elizabeth admitting that he did separate Jane and Bingley and giving his reasons. In it, he also tells her that Wickham lied and that Wickham actually tried to elope with Darcy’s little sister Georgiana. The content of his letter makes Elizabeth doubt her previous beliefs about Mr Darcy, thinking she may have been too prejudiced.

That summer, Lydia gets her parents’ permission to go to Brighton, where she will be near the soldiers, who are leaving Meryton (the local town). Elizabeth goes with her aunt and uncle the Gardiners on a trip, during which she visits Pemberley, Mr Darcy’s estate. Darcy shows up unexpectedly and spends time with the Gardiners and Elizabeth, introducing them to Georgiana and generally showing his best behaviour.

However, the promising trip is cut short by the news that Lydia has eloped with Wickham. Mr Bennet and Mr Gardiner are looking for her, but they have little success. Shortly later, Mr Gardiner sends a letter saying he has found them and convinced the opportunistic Wickham to marry Lydia, in exchange for a small annual income.

Shortly after, Wickham and Lydia pay a visit to the Bennets as a married couple, and Elizabeth discovers that Darcy was really the one who found them and paid Wickham off but made Mr Gardiner take all the credit for it. Mr Bingley then returns to Netherfield Park with Mr Darcy. Having talked to Darcy, Bingley resumes his courtship of Jane and eventually proposes to her.

One day, Lady Catherine angrily comes to see Elizabeth, as she has heard rumours that Elizabeth is secretly engaged to Darcy, and Lady Catherine wants to stop the union because her daughter and Darcy have been promised to each other since childhood as a marriage of convenience. Elizabeth says they are not engaged, but ambiguously adds that she cannot promise she will do anything against her own happiness.

The news of this reply gives Darcy hope, and he proposes to Elizabeth again. This time she accepts, and it ends with a double wedding for Elizabeth and Darcy and Jane and Bingley. Mrs Bennet is extremely relieved that three out of five daughters are now married.
