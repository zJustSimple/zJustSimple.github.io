Trump's presidency

Donald Trump’s presidency began on 20th of January, 2017. During his first months in office, Donald Trump began to act on the promises he made during his campaign, signing several executive orders and planning future policies.

Trump’s promise to increase incomes by making big tax cuts for working and middle-income Americans and for corporations, for example, has been criticized for potentially increasing the national debt. Bigger tax cuts were also planned for companies in the hopes that they would keep their business in the US and create more jobs.

This plan, however, was initially delayed because of the slow progress of replacing the Affordable Care Act (also known as Obamacare). Trump supported repealing Obamacare from the start, promising that his administration would be able to provide better health insurance for all Americans, but in practice it proved very difficult to agree on an alternative plan.

Trump made his anti-immigration and anti-refugees views known from the beginning of his campaign, and he promised to build a large wall along the US-Mexico border in order to stop illegal immigrants. In his first days in office he signed two executive orders designed to implement his plans to restrict immigration. He has also made efforts to ban refugees and travelers from Muslim-majority countries from entering the US, and has put extensive pressure on the Democrats in an effort to secure funding for his wall.

Concerning environmental policies, Trump has generally positioned himself as a denier of climate change. Although he has sometimes admitted its reality, Trump and others in his administration claimed it is naturally occurring and not caused by human activity. This point of view led him to pull the US out of the Paris climate agreement, and reverse several Obama-era restrictions on greenhouse gas emissions.

In terms of foreign policy, bombings in Syria and Afghanistan by the US have made commentators remark that Trump distanced himself from his “America first” campaign agenda, which implied that the US would avoid intervening in international affairs. However, his recent decisions to reduce US military involvement in both Afghanistan and Syria do seem to point in the more isolationist direction that Trump promised in his campaign.

In the following sections, we will provide a more detailed overview of each year of Donald Trump's presidency, covering the most important events and developments. Note that Donald Trump assumed office on January 20, 2017. The first year of his presidency is therefore from January 20, 2017 to January 20, 2018, and so on with the following years. 

Textual perspective 1 - Donald Trump's Inaugural Address
 

Trump made this speech when he took office in January 2017. He paints a dark picture of the current state of the US, but promises a brighter future under his leadership. He states that he will make America great and strong once more.

Textual perspective 2 - " 'Dreamers' Put Their Trust in DACA. What Now?" by Jose Antonio Vargas
 

This opinion article is a criticism of Trump's uncompromising stance on illegal immigration. An illegal immigrant himself, Vargas describes the many difficulties he has faced in his life, and worries that things will become much worse for his fellow immigrants, whose ability to stay in the US are now uncertain.
