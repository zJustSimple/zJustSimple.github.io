Descriptive essay: My grandmother's house

Prin intermediu "My grandmother's house" noi îți propunem o lucrare creativă la limba engleză, concepută de profesorii noștri. Ai ocazia să folosești această resursă edicațională pentru a-ți îmbunătăți aptitudinile scrise la limba engleză, dar și ca sursă de inspirație pentru a obține rezultate foarte bune. 

Această temă, eseu descriptiv despre casa bunicii, a fost propusă pentru clasa a X-a, la Olimpiada Națională din anul 2012, iar lucrarea a fost redactată la un nivel de limbă B2/C1.

Descriptive essay: My grandmother's house

Write a 300-word descriptive essay with the title My Grandmother’s House.

As the larger world constantly expands before our eyes, others diminish with the passing of each day. What was once a world of its own is now a fond memory, encased in the quiet days of my childhood. This was my grandmother’s house.

Back in the day, a family trip to the nearby village where my grandparents lived seemed like a mission to Jupiter. The stark contrast between the crowded and noisy grey of the city and the peaceful green of the countryside had a deep impact on me. To reach the house, you would take the narrow side road and pass under a thick dome of poplars. During summers, the air would be filled with an eerie cloud of white fluff, quite different from the grey haze of city traffic.

This long corridor of green led to the old brick house, which was surrounded by a dark wooden fence. The heavy iron gate always squeaked on its hinges, and for me, that particular sound marked the passing into another world. A white cobblestone path slithered through the tall grass and on to the porch where my grandmother quietly stood, waiting for my arrival.

Like an old and forgotten guardian she stood there, but her eyes shone with green brilliance, inviting me to explore the mysteries that lay hidden inside. The air bore the scent of unfamiliar flowers, and the sharp trills of birds pierced the all-surrounding quiet. I would play in the yard for some time, under her smiling and watchful eyes, until the evening cold would seep through my limbs. Then we would go inside, where another world would open itself to me, ancient and exotic, with places and objects that refused to reveal their immediate purpose.

Such were the days where one could wonder and revel at the magical unfamiliarity of a world forgotten in time; but sadly, those days are long gone, and only a dark, crumbling ruin lies there, alone and unvisited.
