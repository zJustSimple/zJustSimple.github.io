The 2017-2018 North Korean crisis

The crisis began in 2017 when North Korea conducted missile and nuclear tests that demonstrated the country’s ability to launch long-range ballistic missiles. This also suggested that North Korea had the capacity to build a strong nuclear arsenal faster than anticipated by the US.

Tensions rose as North Korea sent an open letter to several nations, describing itself as a nuclear power and threatening with the possibility of nuclear warfare if the US tried to attack North Korea. This was also a reaction to several of US President Donald Trump’s remarks towards North Korean leader Kim-Jong-un, which were interpreted by the leader as offensive and threatening.

However, by 2018, North Korea improved its diplomatic relations with South Korea, which led to a halt of nuclear and missile tests in North Korea and later culminated with the signing of the Panmunjom Declaration for Peace. In the declaration, the leaders of North and South Korea promise to work together on ending the Korean conflict and commit to the complete nuclear disarmament of the Korean Peninsula.

Moreover, a historical meeting took place between US President Donald Trump and North Korea Leader Kim Jong-un in June 2018. This was the first meeting between leaders of North Korea and of the US. The leaders signed a joint statement, calling for full denuclearization of the Korean Peninsula and committing to peaceful relations between the two nations. However, later intelligence data gathered by the US has caused some suspicion that North Korea may not have abandoned their efforts to construct nuclear missiles entirely. 

The Iran nuclear deal

Iran’s nuclear capabilities have long been a reason for international concern, although the country has repeatedly denied that it is building or is in possession of nuclear weapons.

However, Iran has developed a nuclear program over the years, which affected its relationship with the West. In 2015, a deal was reached between Iran and the West. In the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA, also known as the Iran deal) Iran pledged to limit its nuclear activities, in return to getting relief from economic sanctions.

In 2016 and 2017, Iran tested three ballistic missiles. The US reacted by applying sanctions to several companies and individuals connected to the ballistic missiles program.

The relationship between Iran and the US became tenser after Donald Trump criticized both Iran and the JCPOA in an address to the UN General Assembly in September, 2017. A month later, Trump placed new sanctions on several entities connected with Iran’s ballistic missile programs.

In May 2008, despite pressure from France, Germany, and the United Kingdom, which were key negotiators of the Iran deal, President Trump announced the United States’ withdrawal from the Iran deal and that he will impose new strict economic sanctions against Iran.

In September 2018, the European Union announced its plan to save the Iran deal and provide solutions for US' financial sanctions. In turn, President Trump declared that the US is against the plan and called for Iran’s economic isolation. Moreover, US sanctions threatened to limit Iran’s ability to sell oil, the main resource in Iran.

Although Trump’s strict policy placed the US against both Iran and Europe, some say that the US might be successful in its goal to cut off Iran from the rest of the world. This is because, once confronted with a choice between dealing with the US and dealing with Iran, large businesses would prefer to remain on the US market.
