Barack Obama's "Yes We Can" speech
  1. Engleză
  2. Barack Obama's "Yes We Can" speech

Summary

Barack Obama begins his “Yes We Can” speech by thanking the audience and congratulating Hillary Clinton for winning the elections in New Hampshire.

He continues to argue that the fact that he came so far in the electoral race is a victory as he was not the likeliest candidate. He claims that this was possible thanks to the millions of people who mobilized and cast their vote for him because they want change. 

He argues that regardless of people’s background, their decision to vote is a sign they want to take the country in a new direction, to show their unity and challenge political divisions, to end unaffordable health care, and to challenge the power of the drug and insurance industries.

Obama continues to state his political platform, which includes tax cuts for the middle class, improving education and teachers’ salaries, promoting environmentally friendly agriculture, research and business, and ending the war in Iraq and taking care of veterans.

He criticizes politicians who have used the tragedy of 9/11 to get votes but argues that all politicians in the race share the same goals of facing contemporary challenges such as terrorism, climate change, poverty, and disease.

He then thanks the voters, organizers, and campaigners who aided his success in the electoral campaign.

He rejects those who don’t believe his policies can succeed, as well as those who argue that he gives false hope to people. Obama argues that hope is what defines the American spirit. To support this view, he gives the examples of the Declaration of Independence, immigrants who came to the US, workers’ unions, women’s rights activists, and the civil rights movements, as well as the achievement of landing on the moon.

He ends by talking about his campaign in the South and West, claiming that people across the country share similar struggles even though they might seem different. He argues for unity in the US for bringing about positive change, restating the message at the heart of his campaign: yes we can.
