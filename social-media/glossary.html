Glossary

On this page you can find words that are particularly useful to know when studying the topic of social media. Click each word for a full definition in English.

addiction (s)
 

A persistent and compulsive use of a substance or item. In this context, it refers to compulsive use of social media platforms.

algorithm (s)
 

A set of steps that are followed in order to complete a computer process. In this context, it often refers to the way a program processes user information in order to show the user relevant suggestions.

chat room (s)
 

A site or program that allows people to send messages to each other, often publicly or related to a specific topic.

comment (s/v)
 

A social media feature that allows users to interact with a post and express their opinion. Often there is also a feature which allows responses to specific comments, allowing long conversations to take place.

cyberattack (s)
 

An attempt to gain illegal access to a computer or system, usually for the purpose of causing damage or stealing data.

cyberbullying (s)
 

Bullying carried out through the use of electronic communication such as comments or instant messages.  

data leak (s)
 

A situation in which people learn about information that is supposed to be secret. Sometimes data is leaked directly by the company responsible for the data, but it may also happen accidentally or because of cyberattacks (see above). 

echo chamber (s)
 

A closed system where bias or personal beliefs are reinforced by the repetition of the same idea. Social media platforms often use algorithms to suggest content and posts to users based on their interests and political points of view - which means that someone who likes a lot of far-right political content, for instance, is going to see even more of that kind of content. This can give a person the false impression that his or her viewpoint is shared more widely than it is. 

forum (s)
 

An online discussion site where people can hold conversations in the form of posted messages.

hashtag (s)
 

A word or phrase that starts with the symbol # and classifies or explains the accompanying text. Hashtags are used to organise and group information online. 

hate speech (s)
 

Speech meant to attack a person or a group often based on their race, religion, sexual orientation, etc. Social media is a powerful way to spread hate speech, but recently there has been more pressure on social media platforms to introduce measures to fight it. 

influencer (s)
 

A person, usually a celebrity, with a large number of followers on various social media platforms who is able to influence the opinion and behaviour of their followers with their recommendations. Often influencers have sponsorship deals which are not fully disclosed, leading to issues of hidden advertising. 

internet troll (s/v)
 

A person who intentionally antagonises others online by posting offensive comments or other disruptive content. Can also be used as a verb, referring to the act of deliberately creating disruptive comments or content. 

instant-messaging (s)
 

A system for sending messages quickly over the Internet. Sometimes shortened to IM. 

like (s/v)
 

A common social media feature that allows users to interact with a post and show their approval. Often associated with a thumbs-up symbol or a heart.

livestream (s)
 

Video or audio that is broadcast in real time over the internet.

mainstream media (s)
 

A term used to refer collectively to media that reaches, influences, and is consumed by a large number of people, reflecting and shaping current trends and popular attitudes.

microblogging (s)
 

A form of blogging that generates content that is limited in size, for instance short texts or videos.

newsfeed (s)
 

Also referred to as “feed”. A social media feature that presents updates from social media accounts followed by a user. Newsfeeds may also contain large amounts of advertising, which may be more or less obvious. 

polarisation (s)
 

Division into two opposites. In this context, it generally refers to political polarisation, which happens when people support opposing political parties or ideologies. This can happen when social media platforms become echo chambers (see above) for certain political views.

share (s/v)
 

A feature on various social media platforms that allows users to distribute content on the platform or on other social media sites. Often this feature means that users are effectively providing free advertising for companies.

social media (s)
 

Forms of electronic communication through which users create online communities to share information, ideas, personal messages, and other content.

trend (v/s)
 

To generate or attract a lot of interest or attention, especially online and on social media. In this context, the term ‘trending’ is used to refer to topics that have become popular on social media because many users posted about them in a short time.

thread (s)
 

A series of electronic messages or comments that follow a single topic or that respond to a single message. In this context, it refers to discussions between users in group chats dedicated to a certain topic.

tweet (s/v)
 

A post made on Twitter. Tweets were originally restricted to 140 characters, but this limit was later expanded to 280 characters. 

user-generated (adj)
 

Any form of content, such as images, videos, text, and audio, that have been posted by users of online platforms, as opposed to professional journalists or content-creators.

verified (adj)
 

An account on a social media site, usually belonging to a celebrity, company, or institution that has been verified as being authentic. Accounts are generally verified by the social media provider.

viral (adj)
 

Quickly and widely spread or popularised, especially through being shared on social media.

viral marketing (s)
 

A form of advertising where companies create content on social media in the hopes that consumers will start sharing it among themselves, resulting in highly efficient marketing for very little cost.
