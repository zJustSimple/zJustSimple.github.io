Introduction

Over the past decade, social media has experienced a rapid rise in popularity and has become part of the daily life of millions of people. In the United States, for example, the social media usage among adults has increased from 5% in 2005 to 69% in 2018. 

Facebook and YouTube are the social media sites with the widest international usage. They are followed in popularity by WhatsApp, Instagram, WeChat, QQ, LinkedIn, Twitter, and other social media platforms.

Below, we will give a brief description of some of the most important social media platforms. Note that Facebook and YouTube are so important that we have given them individual subpages. 

Twitter

Twitter was created in 2006 by Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams. It distinguished itself from other social media platforms by adopting a format that only allowed users to write short posts. The initial limit was 140 characters, but after 2017, Twitter doubled the character limits in all languages except Chinese, Japanese, and Korean. Twitter gained a lot of popularity because it offers different possibilities from other social media platforms. For example, Twitter is widely used for microblogging.

Tweets are publicly visible by default, but users can restrict the privacy settings of their profiles so that their tweets can be visible only to their followers. Registered users can post, like, and retweet (share) tweets. Users can mention other users in their tweets or reply to others. They can also group posts together by topic or type by using hashtags. The frequent use of a hashtag by numerous users in a short time causes the topic to trend. The Twitter web interface displays a list of trending topics on the sidebar of the home page, meaning that some people use Twitter as a source of breaking news and updates.

Twitter is also widely used for political debates. Many politicians, such as the US President Donald Trump, are notorious for using the platform to promote their political views or personal opinions. These tweets quickly spread on the platform, enabling other users to engage in a debate and voice their own views. The tweets are also often picked up by the media and included in news articles. However, political tweets also stir controversies and attract trolls.

LinkedIn

LinkedIn was founded in 2002 by Reid Hoffman and others, and it was one of the first social media sites to be created. The site focuses on professional networking, allowing people to connect with companies, business colleagues, university classmates, etc. It is mainly used by companies to post vacant job positions, while regular users use the site to seek employment opportunities and to network with other people in the professional field. Consequently, LinkedIn became a powerful business service, and about 80% of companies in the United States use it for the recruiting process.

LinkedIn allows both employees and employers to create profiles. Employee profiles may contain their CVs where they describe their work experience, education and training, interests, etc. An employer’s profile may contain information about the company or job details. LinkedIn limits what a user may be able to view or share based on whether or not they have a paid account. Users can form connections with one another that act like friend requests. LinkedIn allows users to opt out of displaying their connections to the public.

Instagram

Instagram was created in 2010 by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger. It was initially available only for devices with an iOS operating system, but a version for Android devices was later launched in 2012. In the same year, Instagram was acquired by Facebook.

Instagram is mainly used for uploading photos and videos. These can be edited by selecting various filters or modifying the brightness, contrast, saturation, etc. Photos and videos can also be organised by using tags or location information. Adding any of these two to a public post also increases its reach, as the post will be included in the search results related to the tag or location. An account's posts can be shared publicly or only with the user’s followers. Users can see trending content via the Explore feature that displays popular posts and stories.

Initially, the aspect ratio of the uploaded content was limited to 1:1 so that all photos and videos were square framed. However, this limitation was removed in 2015. The platform also introduced the ability to include multiple images or videos in a single post. Users can like and comment on the posts, and they can follow other users to add their content to their feed. Instagram also allows messaging features, stories, and livestreams. The web interface, however, is feature-limited as, for examples, users cannot upload stories via the web interface. 

Instagram is particularly popular among young people who often see other platforms like Facebook as outdated. In 2018, 71% of Americans aged 18 to 24 reported they used Instagram. Instagram is currently owned by Facebook.

Snapchat

Snapchat was created in 2011 by Evan Spiegel, Bobby Murphy, and Reggie Brown. Snapchat is primarily used for creating and sending multimedia messages that usually consist of a photo or short video and a text caption. The message can be edited to include filters, effects, drawings, etc.

Snapchat’s main feature is that the messages are only available for a short amount of time before they disappear and become inaccessible to view. This feature was designed by the creators as a response to worries about the permanent nature of other social media content. This means that Snapchat users do not have to worry about their messages remaining visible on the platform for a long time and affecting their life later. However, people can still take screenshots of messages and post them on other platforms. Usually, the users receive a notification if a screenshot was taken of the content they sent, but there are also ways to save it in secret.

The idea behind Snapchat was also meant to encourage users to create more natural and emotional content rather than the staged or doctored content often posted on other platforms where posts remain visible for a long time. The innovative concept and the accessible interface became widely popular among young people. In 2018, 78% of Americans aged 18 to 24 said they use Snapchat, out of which 71% said they visit the platform multiple times a day.
