The impact of social media in other nations

People's rising access to the Internet worldwide facilitated the use of social media. This has a significant impact on people's lives around the globe.

India

In India, social media is often used to spread hate speech and fake news, especially through WhatsApp, an instant messaging service owned by Facebook. Here, messages about natural disasters, conspiracy theories, and health hazards are often circulated, occasionally causing mass hysteria among parts of the population.

These stories often lead to tensions between castes and religions, instigating discrimination and propagating hate speech. For example, in 2017, a video depicting two people being decapitated was shared on Facebook, with a caption claiming it depicted Indian soldiers being killed in Pakistan. However, the footage was in fact from a gang war in Brazil. In another instance, several fake images were shared on Twitter and captioned as presenting the violent attacks of Muslim Rohingya people against Hindus. In both cases, the posts were used to spread radical nationalist beliefs that called for the exclusion and persecution of Muslims in India.

There have also been examples of fake news that have led to the deaths of several people. In 2017, rumours about child abductors in a village in Jharkhand were spread through WhatsApp messages and prompted villagers to attack strangers. Seven people were beaten to death as a result and locals also clashed with policemen in multiple areas.

Myanmar

In Myanmar, news reports revealed that Facebook was used by military officials as a tool to incite lynchings against the Rohingya Muslim minority by facilitating the spread of fake news and hate speech against them.

In 2012, a series of posts on Facebook created by the Buddhist majority referred to the Muslim minorities in derogatory terms and accused them of crimes that jeopardised Myanmar’s national security. The posts also incited to the punishment and banishment of Rohingya people. The campaign led to discrimination and mass murder, as Rohingya settlements were burned down and many people were killed.

At that time, Facebook had different review policies from the ones it has today and, as a consequence, the content was easily shared on the platform without being detected or blocked. However, Facebook has since deleted accounts of Myanmar military figures accused of inciting violence.

United Kingdom

During the referendum calling for the withdrawal of the United Kingdom from the European Union in 2016 (Brexit), social media had a major impact on political polarisation. Voters were polarised between those who wanted the United Kingdom to leave the European Union and those who wanted the United Kingdom to remain.

Social media websites offered the perfect environment for the creation of echo chambers, where users kept gathering and sharing information that matched with their own political view. “Leave” and “Remain” echo chambers were formed especially on Facebook and Twitter. This was caused especially by the recommendation algorithms supported by the sites, which presented the users with the same type of information they often interacted with, often leaving users unaware that they were not seeing the full scope of the situation or other sides of the argument.

Reliance on digital technology

Since the creation of the Internet and the development of communication technologies, people have increasingly started relying on technology to engage with each other and the world around them. Digital technology has evolved from being a utility used when the need arises to being part of almost every aspect of daily life.

People rely on digital technology to administer their work, their finances, their household, and even their personal relationships. This reliance has led to the increase in popularity of online banking and e-commerce that allow people to easily access their funds and acquire products and services without having to travel or book appointments. For their emotional well-being, people can turn to online therapy. For example, many mobile applications teach meditation methods and help users keep track of their progress. Also, people can turn to dating sites to find a romantic partner.

Also, digital technology helps people keep up with the constant changes of the world. However, digital technology also fuels these worldwide changes. With the amount of development, innovation, and information currently available, it would be almost impossible to stay updated without the use of technology. Therefore, people use digital technology to connect with products, people, and information from anywhere in the world.  

However, since access to the Internet is widely available, people sometimes get disconnected from time and reality. As technology fails or takes too much time to complete a task, people tend to immediately get frustrated. This is because the rhythm of our world has changed, becoming more rapid as technology has made the production process easier and shorter. For this reason, many people report having a shorter attention span and becoming irritated more easily.

The distinction between digital and non-digital interaction also became blurred. This became particularly true with the development of virtual reality, which combines both digital and non-digital elements. However, this type of technology is highly innovative and has many benefits. For example, from a creative point of view, virtual reality can be used to create digital art in three dimensions.

Nowadays, digital technology also provides people with a more personalised experience based on their preferences and behaviour when using technology. This makes technology highly addictive because it caters to individual interests and fuels obsessive tendencies. This has significant implications for clinical practice because unlike other addictions, Internet addiction, for example, is very hard to treat and control. This is because total abstinence from using the Internet is hard to achieve when the Internet is such an integral element of today’s culture. For example, most people have to use the Internet for work or school.
