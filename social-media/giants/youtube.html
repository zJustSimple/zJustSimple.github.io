Launch and development

YouTube was created in 2005 by Chad Hurley, Steve Chen, and Jawed Karim, who previously worked for the online payment company PayPal. The intention of the creators was to launch a community video-sharing site that could serve as an alternative to television.

YouTube was revolutionary in the social media world because it offered a simple interface at a time when posting and viewing videos online was a difficult task. The site started out as a public beta version and went through several interface changes until it was officially launched.

The site gained immediate popularity and, in 2006, YouTube signed its first deal with three major music labels. Later that year, the site was acquired by Google. The company announced that, in 2006, more than 65,000 new videos were being uploaded every day, and that the site was receiving 100 million video views per day.

In 2011, the site radically changed its interface to resemble television. YouTube introduced channels on its homepage, directing the users’ navigation towards channels for entertainment, sports, music, news, etc. The users could also now subscribe to these channels.

In 2018, 73% of the adult population of the United States reported they used YouTube, it is especially popular among people aged 18 to 24. In 2018, YouTube had over 1.9 billion users worldwide.

Main features and services

YouTube is belongs to the type of social media focused on user-generated content because enables users to upload and view original content. YouTube acts as a major distribution platform for content creators and advertisers where they can share movie clips, music videos, video blogs, original videos, etc. YouTube also has a social function because it provides a place where people can connect with each other and inform and inspire other users through the content they upload or the feedback they leave.

The main feature of YouTube is video upload. By default, users can upload videos that are up to 15 minutes long. However, verified users can upload videos with a maximum length of 12 hours. Users can also upload 3D videos and 360-degree videos. Videos can be organised into playlists that can be made available for public view or kept private. Users can choose the quality and format of the videos they upload or watch. They can also leave comments, like or dislike the video, subscribe to the channel that posted the video to receive updates, and share the video on other social media platforms.

Another major feature of YouTube is livestreaming. This feature is available only to verified users and is usually used to stream video games, host live Q&A sessions, or hold lectures.

Like Facebook, YouTube also relies on advertising to produce revenue. Short advertisements are usually presented at the beginning, middle or end of a video. Advertisements are sometimes based on the content of the video, but are also typically tailored to the individual user. The site offers YouTube Premium, a subscription service, as an advertising-free alternative.

Controversy

A part of Youtube’s users disregard the site’s policies and community guidelines. For example, some users illegally upload music or movies created by others, violating copyright. However, original content can also be problematic as, for example, many videos on YouTube contain controversial content like inappropriate videos directed towards children, or videos that promote terrorism, hate speech, conspiracy theories, etc.

While YouTube has policies on harassment and bullying, the guidelines do not explicitly prevent creators from uploading videos featuring conspiracy theories or misleading information. YouTube has made attempts to moderate this type of content, and many videos were taken down and channels distributing this type of content have been deactivated. However, these decisions are often arbitrary, as there are no clear guidelines. Therefore, some videos may be taken down while others may not be. YouTube’s ban regulations are also flawed, as YouTube bans accounts if they receive three warnings within a three-month period. However, channels can still publish dubious content over longer periods of time. Some content might even be wrongfully removed. Many content creators on YouTube have complained that the platform removes their videos or excludes them from advertising revenue for unclear or inconsistent reasons, and have called for the company's policies to be more transparent in the future.
